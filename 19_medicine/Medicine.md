# 19. Medicine (MD/DO Programs)

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Pre-medical preparation | Any major plus biology, chemistry, organic chemistry, physics, biochemistry, statistics, writing, psychology, sociology |
| Professional degree | MD, DO |
| Combined programs | MD/PhD, MD/MPH, MD/MBA, MD/MSCR |
| Postgraduate training | Residency, fellowship, board certification |

**Note:** Medicine is a professional pathway organized around preclinical science, supervised clinical training, residency specialization, and lifelong continuing education. Research depth usually comes through academic medicine, physician-scientist tracks, or formal graduate training.

---

## 19.1 Core Curriculum

### Pre-medical foundations

| Area | Standard preparation | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Biology | Intro biology, genetics, cell biology, physiology | Base for pathology, pharmacology, organ systems |
| Chemistry | General chemistry, organic chemistry, biochemistry | Drug mechanisms, metabolism, diagnostics |
| Physics | Mechanics, electricity/magnetism, optics | Imaging, cardiopulmonary physiology, instrumentation |
| Behavioral science | Psychology, sociology | MCAT, patient behavior, social determinants |
| Quantitative skills | Statistics, biostatistics, basic study design | Evidence appraisal and clinical reasoning |

### Medical school core

| Phase | Core elements |
|---|---|
| **Pre-clinical years** | Anatomy, histology, physiology, pathology, pharmacology, microbiology, immunology, neuroscience, genetics, ethics, epidemiology, clinical skills |
| **Integrated systems blocks** | Cardiovascular, pulmonary, renal, GI, endocrine, reproductive, hematology/oncology, neurology/psychiatry, musculoskeletal, dermatology |
| **Clerkships** | Internal medicine, surgery, pediatrics, obstetrics/gynecology, psychiatry, family medicine, neurology, emergency medicine |
| **Advanced year** | Sub-internships, ICU/emergency exposure, acting internships, electives, research |

## 19.2 Graduate Depth (Residency, Fellowship, Academic Medicine)

| Stage | Main focus |
|---|---|
| Residency | High-volume supervised diagnosis, management, procedures, triage, communication, care coordination |
| Fellowship | Subspecialty depth: cardiology, oncology, critical care, GI, endocrinology, infectious disease, rheumatology, etc. |
| Academic medicine | Clinical trials, translational medicine, outcomes research, guideline development, medical education |
| Physician-scientist path | Protected research time, grant writing, lab or clinical research leadership |

### Core graduate competencies
- Diagnostic reasoning under uncertainty
- Evidence-based medicine and guideline use
- Safe prescribing and deprescribing
- Procedural competence appropriate to specialty
- Team-based care, quality improvement, systems thinking
- Clinical research literacy and trial interpretation

## 19.3 Key Methods & Tools

| Domain | Core tools |
|---|---|
| History & exam | Structured interviewing, physical exam, differential diagnosis |
| Diagnostics | CBC/CMP, cultures, pathology, ECG, EEG, ultrasound, CT, MRI, PET, endoscopy |
| Evidence use | Trial appraisal, systematic reviews, guidelines, risk calculators, NNT/NNH |
| Informatics | EHRs, clinical decision support, PACS, e-prescribing, telemedicine |
| Procedures | Airway management, vascular access, biopsy, endoscopy, image-guided intervention, surgery |
| Quality & safety | Root-cause analysis, care pathways, checklists, antimicrobial stewardship |

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Medical education and evidence-based medicine
- Flexner A. 1910. *Medical Education in the United States and Canada*.
- Cochrane AL. 1972. *Effectiveness and Efficiency*.
- Sackett DL et al. 1996. "Evidence based medicine: what it is and what it isn't." *BMJ*.
- Cook DA, West CP. 2012. "Conducting systematic reviews in medical education." *Medical Education*.
- Epstein RM, Hundert EM. 2002. "Defining and assessing professional competence." *JAMA*.
- Crossing the Quality Chasm. 2001. National Academies report.

#### Epidemiology, screening, and risk
- Doll R, Hill AB. 1950. "Smoking and carcinoma of the lung." *BMJ*.
- Wilson JMG, Jungner G. 1968. *Principles and Practice of Screening for Disease*.
- Framingham Heart Study investigators. 1948-present. Foundational cardiovascular cohort work.
- Rose G. 1985. "Sick individuals and sick populations." *International Journal of Epidemiology*.
- Feinstein AR. 1967. *Clinical Judgment*.
- Grimes DA, Schulz KF. 2002. "Bias and causal associations in observational research." *Lancet*.

#### Cardiology and vascular medicine
- ISIS-2 Collaborative Group. 1988. Aspirin and streptokinase in acute MI. *Lancet*.
- GUSTO Investigators. 1993. Accelerated tPA vs streptokinase. *NEJM*.
- Scandinavian Simvastatin Survival Study. 1994. Statins in CAD. *Lancet*.
- HOPE Study Investigators. 2000. Ramipril in high-risk patients. *NEJM*.
- COURAGE Trial. 2007. PCI vs optimal medical therapy in stable CAD. *NEJM*.
- EMPA-REG OUTCOME. 2015. Empagliflozin and cardiovascular outcomes. *NEJM*.

#### Oncology and hematology
- Fisher B et al. 1989. Lumpectomy plus irradiation in breast cancer. *NEJM*.
- Bonadonna G et al. 1976. CMF adjuvant chemotherapy in breast cancer. *NEJM*.
- Druker BJ et al. 2001. Imatinib in chronic myeloid leukemia. *NEJM*.
- Slamon DJ et al. 2001. Trastuzumab plus chemotherapy in HER2 breast cancer. *NEJM*.
- Topalian SL et al. 2012. Anti-PD-1 in cancer. *NEJM*.
- Howlader N et al. SEER-based survival trend analyses as core oncology epidemiology references.

#### Infectious disease and critical care
- Marshall JC et al. 1993. ACCP/SCCM sepsis definitions. *Chest*.
- Rivers E et al. 2001. Early goal-directed therapy. *NEJM*.
- Dellinger RP et al. 2013. Surviving Sepsis Campaign guidelines. *Critical Care Medicine*.
- RECOVERY Collaborative Group. 2021. Dexamethasone in COVID-19. *NEJM*.
- ACTT-1 Study Group. 2020. Remdesivir for COVID-19. *NEJM*.
- WHO Solidarity Trial Consortium. 2021. Repurposed antivirals for COVID-19. *NEJM*.

#### Surgery, safety, and systems
- Lister J. 1867. Antiseptic principle in surgery. *Lancet*.
- McBurney C. 1889. Experience with early operative interference in appendicitis. *NY Medical Journal*.
- The VA/NCI NSABP trials on breast-conserving therapy and oncologic surgery remain core surgical evidence landmarks.
- The European Carotid Surgery Trialists' Collaborative Group. 1991. Endarterectomy evidence. *Lancet*.
- Haynes AB et al. 2009. WHO surgical safety checklist. *NEJM*.
- Pronovost P et al. 2006. Central-line infection reduction. *NEJM*.

## 19.5 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Earlier, cheaper, and more equitable diagnosis
- Antimicrobial resistance and stewardship at scale
- Multimorbidity and polypharmacy in aging populations
- Durable cures for metastatic cancer and neurodegeneration
- Better biomarkers and treatment matching in psychiatry
- Integration of AI without worsening bias or overuse
- Climate-sensitive medicine and resilient health systems
