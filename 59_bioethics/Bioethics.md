# 59. Bioethics

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Bioethics tracks, Philosophy / Public Health with bioethics concentration |
| Masters | MA or MBE in Bioethics, dual degrees with medicine, law, public health |
| PhD | Bioethics, Philosophy with bioethics focus, Health Policy & Ethics |

---

## 59.1 Core Curriculum

### Core Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Foundations of Bioethics** | Normative theories, principlism, casuistry, justice, care ethics |
| **Clinical Ethics** | Consent, capacity, surrogate choice, end-of-life, confidentiality |
| **Research Ethics** | Human subjects, Belmont, IRBs, integrity, vulnerable populations |
| **Public Health Ethics** | Liberty, paternalism, resource allocation, population health, global justice |
| **Genetics & Reproduction** | Screening, IVF, embryo selection, gene editing, disability perspectives |
| **Law & Bioethics** | Health law, rights, regulation, courts, institutions |
| **Technology & Neuroethics** | AI, devices, brain data, enhancement, digital health |

---

## 59.2 Specialization Areas

#### A. Clinical Ethics Consultation
- Bedside conflict, ethics committees, communication, moral distress, mediation.

#### B. Research Ethics & Integrity
- Trials, data, authorship, conflicts, global research, community engagement.

#### C. Genetics, Reproduction & ELSI
- Screening, discrimination, heritable editing, reproductive justice.

#### D. AI, Data & Technology Ethics
- Clinical algorithms, privacy, bias, explainability, automation in care.

#### E. Public Health & Global Bioethics
- Pandemic response, allocation, structural inequity, access to medicines.

---

## 59.3 Key Methods & Tools

| Method | Application |
|---|---|
| **Case Analysis** | Structured reasoning around ethically difficult medical scenarios |
| **Normative Argument** | Applying and critiquing principles, duties, virtues, and justice claims |
| **Policy Analysis** | Regulatory design, institutional governance, allocation frameworks |
| **Empirical Bioethics** | Interviews, surveys, deliberation, sociology of medicine |
| **Clinical Observation** | Understanding real decision settings in hospitals and research centers |
| **Interdisciplinary Interpretation** | Integrating medicine, law, philosophy, theology, disability studies |

## 59.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **AI in medicine** - Accountability, explainability, bias, and clinical trust.
- **Genome editing** - Governance, equity, consent, and disability critique.
- **Pandemic ethics and preparedness** - Allocation, restrictions, and global inequity.
- **Brain data and neurotechnology** - Cognitive liberty, enhancement, and mental privacy.
- **Reproductive justice** - Law, race, disability, and access to care.
- **Institutional bioethics** - How ethics consultation and policy operate under resource constraints.
