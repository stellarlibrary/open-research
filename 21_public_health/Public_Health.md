# 21. Public Health

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Public Health, Community Health, Global Health, Health Sciences |
| Professional masters | MPH, MSPH, MHA |
| Research masters/doctorate | MS/PhD in Epidemiology, Biostatistics, Health Policy, Environmental Health, Social & Behavioral Sciences |
| Practice doctorate | DrPH |

**Note:** Public health is population-level health science and practice. It combines epidemiology, quantitative methods, environmental and social determinants, health systems, and implementation.

---

## 21.1 Core Curriculum

| Core area | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Epidemiology | Measures of disease, study design, bias, confounding, causal inference | Gordis - *Epidemiology* |
| Biostatistics | Probability, inference, regression, survival, longitudinal analysis | Rosner - *Fundamentals of Biostatistics* |
| Environmental health | Air, water, toxicants, occupational health, climate | Frumkin - *Environmental Health* |
| Health policy and management | Systems, financing, organization, quality, leadership | Shi & Singh - *Delivering Health Care in America* |
| Social and behavioral sciences | Behavior change, health communication, disparities | Glanz, Rimer & Viswanath - *Health Behavior* |
| Global health | Disease burden, health systems, maternal-child health, infectious disease | Skolnik - *Global Health 101* |

### Graduate depth
- Epidemiology: infectious, chronic, reproductive, environmental, social, molecular
- Biostatistics: trials, missing data, causal inference, high-dimensional data
- Health policy: economics, access, quality, payment, implementation
- Environmental health: exposure science, climate and health, justice
- Global health: systems strengthening, governance, intervention delivery

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Foundations of epidemiology
- Snow J. 1855. *On the Mode of Communication of Cholera*.
- Doll R, Hill AB. 1950. Smoking and lung cancer. *BMJ*.
- Kannel WB et al. 1961. Framingham risk-factor papers for coronary disease.
- Hill AB. 1965. "The environment and disease: association or causation?" *Proceedings of the Royal Society of Medicine*.
- Rothman KJ. 1976. Causes. *American Journal of Epidemiology*.
- Rose G. 1985. "Sick individuals and sick populations." *IJE*.
- Greenland S, Pearl J, Robins JM. 1999. Causal diagrams for epidemiologic research. *Epidemiology*.
- Hernán MA. 2018. The C-word and scientific euphemisms. *American Journal of Public Health*.

#### Social determinants and health equity
- Marmot M et al. 1991. Whitehall II baseline findings and social-gradient work.
- Adler NE et al. 1994. SES and health. *American Psychologist*.
- Link BG, Phelan J. 1995. Fundamental cause theory. *Journal of Health and Social Behavior*.
- Krieger N. 1994 and 2012. Ecosocial theory papers and overview. *IJE*.
- Williams DR, Mohammed SA. 2013. Racism and health pathways. *Behavioral Medicine*.
- Braveman P, Gottlieb L. 2014. Social determinants framework. *Public Health Reports*.
- Bailey ZD et al. 2017. Structural racism and health inequities. *Lancet*.

#### Infectious disease and outbreak science
- Kermack WO, McKendrick AG. 1927. SIR model. *Proceedings of the Royal Society A*.
- Anderson RM, May RM. 1979–1991. Classic infectious-disease modeling papers and synthesis.
- Fenner F et al. 1988. Smallpox eradication synthesis.
- Fraser C et al. 2004. Factors making epidemics controllable. *PNAS*.
- Fine P, Eames K, Heymann DL. 2011. "Herd immunity." *Clinical Infectious Diseases*.
- Lipsitch M, Dean NE. 2020. Understanding COVID-19 vaccine efficacy. *NEJM*.
- WHO and CDC surveillance guideline documents remain core practice references.

#### Environmental and occupational health
- Needleman HL et al. 1979. Lead exposure and child IQ. *NEJM*.
- Dockery DW et al. 1993. Six Cities Study. *NEJM*.
- Pope CA et al. 2002. Air pollution and mortality. *JAMA*.
- Basu R, Samet JM. 2002. Heat and mortality review. *Epidemiologic Reviews*.
- Landrigan PJ et al. 2018. Pollution and global health burden. *Lancet*.
- Hales S et al. and later climate-health attribution studies are core environmental-health readings.
- O'Neill MS et al. environmental-justice and climate-vulnerability papers remain important complements.

#### Trials, synthesis, causal methods, and implementation
- Rubin DB. 1974 and later. Potential-outcomes framework papers.
- Glasgow RE et al. 1999. RE-AIM framework. *American Journal of Public Health*.
- Moher D et al. 2009. PRISMA statement. *PLoS Medicine*.
- Schulz KF, Altman DG, Moher D. 2010. CONSORT statement. *BMJ*.
- Proctor EK et al. 2011. Implementation outcomes framework. *Administration and Policy in Mental Health*.
- Higgins JPT et al. Cochrane methods papers and handbook as standard synthesis references.
- Damschroder LJ et al. 2009. CFIR. *Implementation Science*.
- Frieden TR. 2010. Health impact pyramid. *American Journal of Public Health*.

#### Global health and health systems
- Alma-Ata Declaration. 1978.
- Murray CJL, Lopez AD. 1996. Global Burden of Disease synthesis.
- World Health Report 2000. Health-systems performance.
- Victora CG et al. 2003. Evidence-based child survival interventions. *Lancet*.
- Jamison DT et al. 2013. *Disease Control Priorities* synthesis papers.
- Farmer P et al. 2013. Reimagining global health delivery. *Lancet*.
- Kruk ME et al. 2018. High-quality health systems. *Lancet Global Health*.

## 21.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Causal inference from messy real-world data
- Pandemic readiness and trust restoration
- Climate adaptation and environmental justice
- Chronic disease prevention beyond individual behavior models
- Better implementation at system scale
- Precision public health without surveillance harms
