# Research Plan: University Majors & Graduate Programs

## Overview

A structured research plan organized around real university degree programs — the way college students, master's students, and PhD candidates actually encounter knowledge. Each entry represents a recognized major or graduate concentration that you can find across top universities worldwide.

The structure follows: **School/College → Department → Majors & Graduate Concentrations**

---

## I. Natural Sciences & Mathematics

### 1. Mathematics
- **Undergrad Majors:** Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Statistics
- **Graduate/PhD:** Pure Mathematics (algebra, analysis, topology, geometry, number theory), Applied Mathematics (numerical analysis, dynamical systems, optimization), Statistics & Probability, Actuarial Science, Mathematical Logic & Foundations

### 2. Physics
- **Undergrad Majors:** Physics, Applied Physics, Engineering Physics
- **Graduate/PhD:** Condensed Matter Physics, Particle & Nuclear Physics, Astrophysics, Quantum Physics, Plasma Physics, Optics & Photonics, Biophysics, Mathematical Physics

### 3. Chemistry
- **Undergrad Majors:** Chemistry, Biochemistry
- **Graduate/PhD:** Organic Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, Physical Chemistry, Analytical Chemistry, Materials Chemistry, Theoretical & Computational Chemistry, Chemical Biology

### 4. Biology
- **Undergrad Majors:** Biology, Molecular Biology, Ecology & Evolutionary Biology, Microbiology, Genetics, Zoology, Botany/Plant Biology, Marine Biology
- **Graduate/PhD:** Cell & Molecular Biology, Genetics & Genomics, Developmental Biology, Neurobiology, Immunology, Microbiology & Virology, Ecology & Conservation Biology, Evolutionary Biology, Systems Biology, Computational Biology & Bioinformatics

### 5. Earth & Environmental Sciences
- **Undergrad Majors:** Geology, Environmental Science, Atmospheric Science/Meteorology, Geophysics, Oceanography
- **Graduate/PhD:** Geochemistry, Paleontology, Seismology, Climate Science, Hydrology, Planetary Science, Remote Sensing & GIS

### 6. Astronomy
- **Undergrad Majors:** Astronomy, Astrophysics, Astronomy & Physics (combined)
- **Graduate/PhD:** Observational Astronomy, Stellar Astrophysics, Galactic Astronomy, Cosmology, Planetary Science, Astrobiology, Gravitational Wave Astronomy

## Research Approach Per Major

For each of the 60 programs:

1. **Core Curriculum** - What undergrads must learn (foundational courses, textbooks)
2. **Graduate Depth** - What changes at the MS/PhD level (specialization, original research)
3. **Key Methods & Tools** - Labs, fieldwork, computation, clinical rotations, studios, etc.
4. **Foundational Texts** - Essential textbooks and landmark papers
5. **Open Problems & Frontiers** - Active research questions in PhD programs
6. **Career Paths** - Academia, industry, professional practice

## Notes

- This list reflects majors as seen at comprehensive research universities (R1 institutions). Smaller colleges may combine or omit some programs.
- Professional degrees (MD, JD, PharmD, DVM, DDS) are post-baccalaureate and have distinct structures from PhD programs.
- Many universities are adding new interdisciplinary majors (e.g., Quantum Engineering, Climate Science, AI/ML as standalone). Category XII will grow.
- Some fields appear in multiple places (e.g., Neuroscience under Biology and as its own interdisciplinary entry) because they exist as both departmental sub-fields and standalone programs at different schools.
