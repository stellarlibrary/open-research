# 25. Veterinary Medicine

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Pre-veterinary preparation | Biology, chemistry, organic chemistry, physics, animal science, statistics |
| Professional degree | DVM, VMD |
| Postgraduate training | Internship, residency, board certification |
| Graduate research | Comparative biomedical sciences, pathobiology, epidemiology, nutrition |

**Note:** Veterinary medicine combines comparative anatomy and physiology, species-specific diagnosis and treatment, herd and population health, food systems, and One Health.

---

## 25.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Anatomy | Comparative domestic anatomy across species | Dyce, Sack & Wensing - *Textbook of Veterinary Anatomy* |
| Physiology | Organ systems across species | Klein - *Cunningham's Textbook of Veterinary Physiology* |
| Pathology | General and systemic pathology, necropsy | Zachary - *Pathologic Basis of Veterinary Disease* |
| Internal medicine | Small animal diagnosis and treatment | Ettinger, Feldman & Côté - *Textbook of Veterinary Internal Medicine* |
| Large animal medicine | Ruminant and equine disease | Radostits et al. - *Veterinary Medicine* |
| Surgery | Soft tissue and orthopedic surgery | Fossum - *Small Animal Surgery* |
| Pharmacology | Species-specific dosing and toxicities | Riviere & Papich - *Veterinary Pharmacology and Therapeutics* |
| Drug reference | Practical dosing and formulations | Plumb - *Plumb's Veterinary Drug Handbook* |
| Epidemiology/public health | Zoonoses, surveillance, food safety, herd health | Thrusfield - *Veterinary Epidemiology* |

### Graduate depth
- Small animal specialties
- Equine and food animal medicine
- Comparative pathology and lab animal medicine
- Preventive medicine, epidemiology, public health
- Wildlife, zoo, and conservation medicine

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Comparative medicine and pathology
- Virchow R. 1858 onward. Comparative pathology writings that shaped One Health thinking.
- Schwabe CW. 1964. *Veterinary Medicine and Human Health*.
- Zachary and comparative pathology review papers remain core pathology anchors.
- Jubb, Kennedy, and Palmer case-defining pathology literature remains foundational.
- Comparative oncology trial network overview papers.
- Paoloni M, Khanna C. 2008. Comparative oncology perspective. *Nat Rev Cancer*.

#### Infectious disease, epidemiology, and One Health
- Thrusfield M. methodological epidemiology papers and text-based standards.
- Cleaveland S et al. 2001. Diseases from wildlife and livestock to humans. *Philosophical Transactions B*.
- Jones KE et al. 2008. Global trends in emerging infectious diseases. *Nature*.
- Zinsstag J et al. 2011. Mainstreaming One Health. *Preventive Veterinary Medicine*.
- WOAH/OIE terrestrial code documents as core population-health references.
- AVMA/CDC guidance papers on zoonoses and biosecurity.

#### Surgery, anesthesia, and critical care
- Fossum TW landmark small-animal surgery chapters and evidence reviews remain standard.
- Hall LW, Clarke KW and later veterinary anesthesia reference papers.
- RECOVER initiative. 2012 and updates. Veterinary CPR guidelines. *J Vet Emerg Crit Care*.
- Boag AK and related ECC studies on fluid therapy, sepsis, and trauma.
- Johnston SA. canine orthopedic surgery outcome papers.
- ACVECC consensus statements remain major practice references.

#### Internal medicine and therapeutics
- Ettinger and Feldman disease-defining reviews remain curricular anchors.
- IRIS kidney staging guidelines. chronic kidney disease consensus documents.
- ACVIM consensus statements on myxomatous mitral valve disease, pancreatitis, proteinuria, and seizures.
- Sykes JE. infectious disease treatment reviews in small animals.
- Plumb DC and Papich MG dosing/pharmacology review literature.
- ISCAID antimicrobial use guidelines for dogs and cats.

#### Food animal, herd health, and welfare
- Radostits OM classic herd-health and production medicine evidence.
- Ruegg PL. mastitis prevention and milk-quality studies.
- Guss SB and herd reproductive management literature remain standard.
- Grandin T. animal welfare design and handling papers.
- Rollin BE. veterinary ethics and welfare writings.
- WHO/FAO/WOAH AMR reports relevant to livestock medicine.

#### Wildlife, behavior, and emerging frontiers
- Daszak P, Cunningham AA, Hyatt AD. 2000. Emerging infectious diseases of wildlife. *Science*.
- Gilbert M et al. avian influenza ecology papers.
- Miller RE, Fowler ME zoo medicine reference literature.
- Landsberg GM et al. veterinary behavior medicine reviews.
- Cooke SJ and conservation medicine interface papers.
- Papers on chronic wasting disease, white-nose syndrome, and amphibian chytridiomycosis as canonical wildlife disease readings.

## 25.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Antimicrobial stewardship across companion and food-animal practice
- Better pain recognition across species
- One Health surveillance for spillover and food systems
- Comparative oncology and translational trial design
- Financial sustainability and workforce mental health
- Wildlife disease under climate and land-use change
