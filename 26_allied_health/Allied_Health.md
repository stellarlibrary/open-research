# 26. Allied Health

## Degree Programs

| Profession | Typical degree |
|---|---|
| Physical therapy | DPT |
| Occupational therapy | OTD, MOT |
| Speech-language pathology | MS-SLP |
| Audiology | AuD |
| Physician assistant | MPAS, MPA |
| Respiratory therapy | BS, MS |
| Nutrition and dietetics | MS + supervised practice |
| Radiologic and laboratory sciences | AS, BS, MS |
| Genetic counseling | MS |

**Note:** Allied health groups many clinical and diagnostic professions outside medicine, nursing, pharmacy, and dentistry. Programs are practice-heavy and usually combine professional competencies with a more limited but still important research literature.

## 26.1 Core Curriculum

### Common cross-profession core

| Area | Main topics |
|---|---|
| Biomedical sciences | Anatomy, physiology, pathology, pharmacology |
| Clinical reasoning | Assessment, documentation, care planning, referral |
| Evidence-based practice | Study appraisal, outcomes, implementation |
| Communication | Patient education, counseling, interprofessional teamwork |
| Professional practice | Ethics, licensure, reimbursement, regulation |

### Representative profession-specific standards

| Profession | Standard text |
|---|---|
| Physical therapy | Kisner, Colby & Borstad - *Therapeutic Exercise* |

| Respiratory therapy | Cairo - *Pilbeam's Mechanical Ventilation* |

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Rehabilitation and physical therapy
- Nagi SZ. disability model papers.
- Shumway-Cook A, Woollacott M. motor control framework literature.
- Delitto A et al. low back pain clinical practice guidelines. *J Orthop Sports Phys Ther*.
- Dean CM et al. stroke rehabilitation exercise trials.
- Langhorne P et al. organized stroke unit and rehab evidence. *Cochrane* / *Lancet* line of work.
- Bernhardt J et al. AVERT early mobilization trial. *Lancet*.

#### Occupational therapy and function
- Kielhofner G. Model of Human Occupation writings.
- Law M et al. Canadian Model of Occupational Performance papers.
- Trombly CA occupation-based intervention literature.
- Wolf TJ et al. cognitive rehab and executive-function OT studies.
- Coster W and PEDI-related pediatric functioning papers.
- Grajo LC et al. OT systematic reviews on stroke and ADL recovery.

#### Speech-language pathology and audiology
- Broca P. 1861. Speech production lesion papers.
- Wernicke C. 1874. Aphasia localization work.
- Rosenbek JC et al. dysphagia and aspiration scale papers.
- Logemann JA. videofluoroscopic swallowing assessment literature.
- Yairi E, Ambrose NG. developmental stuttering reviews.
- Wilson RH and Katz J key clinical audiology method papers.

#### Nutrition and metabolic care
- Ancel Keys et al. Seven Countries and diet-heart literature.
- Willett WC et al. nutritional epidemiology framework papers.
- Diabetes Prevention Program Research Group. 2002. lifestyle intervention. *NEJM*.
- PREDIMED Investigators. 2013. Mediterranean diet and CV prevention. *NEJM*.
- ASPEN/ESPEN enteral and parenteral nutrition guidelines.
- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics evidence analyses remain core practice references.

#### Respiratory care, PA practice, and diagnostics
- ARDSNet. 2000. Low tidal volume ventilation. *NEJM*.
- Tobin MJ. respiratory physiology and ventilator management papers.
- ATS/ERS pulmonary function testing standards.
- Light RW. pleural effusion criteria papers.
- AAPA and NCCPA competency statements for PA education and practice.
- TeamSTEPPS and interprofessional collaboration literature used across PA and allied-health programs.

#### Health services, disability, and implementation
- WHO. 2001. *International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health*.
- Berwick DM. quality-improvement papers used across practice disciplines.
- Glasgow RE et al. RE-AIM framework. *AJPH*.
- Bodenheimer T et al. chronic care model literature.
- Institute of Medicine. reports on interprofessional education and quality.
- Cott CA. interprofessional collaboration literature in rehabilitation.

## 26.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Stronger evidence bases for heterogeneous rehab interventions
- Tele-rehabilitation and hybrid care quality
- Better access to speech, OT, PT, respiratory, and nutrition services
- AI-assisted diagnostics without deskilling clinicians
- Integration of disability, participation, and equity frameworks
- More scalable assistive and adaptive technologies
