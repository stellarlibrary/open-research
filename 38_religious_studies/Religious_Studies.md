# 38. Religious Studies & Theology

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Religious Studies, Theology, Biblical Studies, Islamic Studies, Jewish Studies |
| Masters | MA in Religious Studies, MTS, MDiv, MA in Theology |
| PhD | Religious Studies, Theology, Biblical Studies, History of Religions |

**Note:** Religious Studies typically approaches religion historically, philologically, anthropologically, and sociologically; Theology often adds constructive or confessional work. Many departments combine both.

## 38.1 Core Curriculum (Undergraduate)

### Core Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Introduction to the Study of Religion** | What counts as religion, comparison, ritual, myth, symbol, secularism, method debates |
| **World Religions** | Historical and contemporary study of major traditions and regional formations |
| **Hebrew Bible / Old Testament** | Texts, composition, interpretation, Ancient Near Eastern context |
| **New Testament / Early Christianity** | Gospels, Paul, canon formation, Roman imperial context |
| **Islam** | Quran, Hadith, law, theology, history, modern movements |
| **South and East Asian Religions** | Hindu, Buddhist, Jain, Sikh, Daoist, Confucian, Shinto traditions |
| **Religion and Society** | Secularization, politics, gender, lived religion, institutions |

## 38.2 Graduate Specialization Areas

#### A. Biblical Studies
- Philology, textual criticism, archaeology, Second Temple Judaism, historical Jesus, Pauline studies.

#### B. History of Christianity & Theology
- Patristics, medieval theology, Reformation, global Christianity, doctrine, constructive theology.

#### C. Islamic Studies
- Quranic studies, Hadith, law, theology, Sufism, empire, modern reform, contemporary politics.

#### D. South / East Asian Religions
- Sanskrit, Pali, Tibetan, Chinese sources; philosophy, ritual, monastic institutions, modernity.

#### E. Religion, Culture & Society
- Lived religion, material religion, media, law, race, gender, migration, secularism.

#### F. Comparative Ethics & Philosophy of Religion
- Comparative moral thought, metaphysics, theodicy, pluralism, religion and science.

---

## 38.3 Key Methods

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Philology** | Working in primary languages such as Hebrew, Greek, Arabic, Sanskrit, Pali, or Classical Chinese |
| **Historical-Critical Method** | Source, form, redaction, reception, canon, institutional history |
| **Ethnography** | Fieldwork on ritual, communities, and lived religion |
| **Comparative Analysis** | Comparing concepts, practices, and institutions across traditions |
| **Hermeneutics** | Theories of interpretation for scripture, doctrine, and practice |
| **Sociology / Anthropology of Religion** | Religion as social institution, practice, symbol, power |
| **Material Religion** | Images, objects, architecture, pilgrimage, media, embodiment |

## 38.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **Secularism and post-secularity** - Whether classical secularization narratives still explain contemporary life.
- **Religion and nationalism** - Renewed attention to majoritarian politics, populism, and civil religion.
- **Digital religion** - Online ritual, authority, platform mediation, and AI-generated devotional content.
- **Decolonizing the field** - Critiques of the "world religions" model and stronger Indigenous-centered frameworks.
- **Religion, ecology, and climate** - Ethical and ritual responses to environmental crisis.
- **Lived religion vs. textual canons** - Balancing everyday practice with elite doctrinal traditions.
