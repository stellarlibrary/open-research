# 22. Nursing

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Entry pathways | BSN, ADN, accelerated BSN |
| Graduate | MSN, post-master's certificates |
| Doctoral | DNP, PhD in Nursing |
| Advanced practice | NP, CRNA, CNM, CNS |

**Note:** Nursing education is built around foundational sciences, supervised clinical care, patient-centered communication, safety, and progressively advanced responsibility. The BSN is the most standard broad preparation; the DNP and PhD diverge toward practice leadership and research.

---

## 22.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Fundamentals of nursing | ADPIE, safety, hygiene, documentation, therapeutic communication | Potter et al. - *Fundamentals of Nursing* |
| Health assessment | History, physical exam, clinical reasoning | Jarvis - *Physical Examination and Health Assessment* |
| Adult health / med-surg | Cardiovascular, respiratory, renal, endocrine, oncology, perioperative care | Hinkle & Cheever - *Brunner & Suddarth's Textbook of Medical-Surgical Nursing* |
| Pharmacology | Medication classes, dosing, administration, adverse effects | Lehne - *Pharmacology for Nursing Care* |
| Maternal-newborn | Pregnancy, labor, postpartum, neonatal care | Lowdermilk et al. - *Maternity & Women's Health Care* |
| Pediatrics | Growth, development, childhood illness, family-centered care | Hockenberry & Wilson - *Wong's Nursing Care of Infants and Children* |
| Psychiatric-mental health | Therapeutic communication, mood, psychosis, substance use | Varcarolis - *Foundations of Psychiatric-Mental Health Nursing* |
| Community/public health | Population health, prevention, home/community care | Stanhope & Lancaster - *Public Health Nursing* |
| Evidence-based practice | Literature appraisal, QI, translation into care | Melnyk & Fineout-Overholt - *Evidence-Based Practice in Nursing & Healthcare* |

### Graduate depth
- NP training: diagnosis, prescribing, longitudinal management
- DNP: implementation, systems leadership, quality, outcomes
- PhD: symptom science, interventions, health equity, care delivery research

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Nursing theory and professional formation
- Nightingale F. 1860. *Notes on Nursing*.
- Peplau HE. 1952. *Interpersonal Relations in Nursing*.
- Henderson V. 1966. *The Nature of Nursing*.
- Orem DE. 1971. *Nursing: Concepts of Practice*.
- Roy C. 1976. Adaptation-model writings.
- Benner P. 1982. "From novice to expert." *AJN*.
- Benner P et al. 2010. *Educating Nurses*.

#### Quality, safety, and systems
- Donabedian A. 1966. Evaluating quality of medical care. *Milbank Memorial Fund Quarterly*.
- Institute of Medicine. 2000. *To Err Is Human*.
- Aiken LH et al. 2002. Hospital nurse staffing and mortality. *JAMA*.
- Needleman J et al. 2002. Nurse staffing and outcomes. *NEJM*.
- Pronovost P et al. 2006. Central-line infection reduction at scale. *NEJM*.
- Institute of Medicine. 2011. *The Future of Nursing*.
- Lake ET et al. Magnet and work-environment studies as core nursing-services literature.
- Kutney-Lee A et al. 2013. Nurse burnout and patient outcomes. *Health Affairs*.
- Hall LH et al. 2016. Burnout and patient safety systematic review. *BMJ Open*.

#### Evidence-based practice and implementation
- Sackett DL et al. 1996. Evidence-based medicine framework. *BMJ*.
- Melnyk BM et al. 2004. EBP competencies and barriers papers.
- Titler MG. 2008. Translation science in nursing. *Worldviews on Evidence-Based Nursing*.
- Damschroder LJ et al. 2009. CFIR. *Implementation Science*.
- White KM et al. Iowa Model update papers.
- Stevens KR. 2013. The impact of evidence-based practice. *Online Journal of Issues in Nursing*.
- SQUIRE guideline papers remain useful for nursing quality-improvement scholarship.

#### Symptom science, chronic illness, and transitions
- Mishel MH. 1988. Uncertainty in illness theory. *Image*.
- Lorig KR et al. 1999. Chronic disease self-management. *Medical Care*.
- Naylor MD et al. 1999 and 2004. Transitional-care-model trials. *JAGS* / *JAMA*.
- Coleman EA et al. 2006. Care transitions intervention. *Archives of Internal Medicine*.
- Dodd M et al. Symptom-cluster literature and related oncology-nursing papers.
- Davidson JE et al. ICU family-centered care statements as major practice references.
- McCorkle R and later symptom-management science papers remain influential nursing research anchors.

#### Mental health, communication, and patient-centered care
- Stewart M et al. Patient-centered medicine papers that shaped communication training.
- McCormack B, McCance T. Person-centred nursing framework papers.
- Watson J. Caring-science writings as a major nursing-humanism reference set.
- Haskard Zolnierek KB, DiMatteo MR. 2009. Communication and adherence meta-analysis. *Medical Care*.
- Starmer AJ et al. 2014. I-PASS handoff intervention. *NEJM*.
- Institute for Healthcare Improvement materials on bedside shift report and communication remain core practice references.

#### Public health, equity, and workforce
- Marmot M. 2005 and 2010 social-gradient reports relevant to nursing.
- Aiken LH et al. 2014. Education mix and hospital mortality across Europe. *Lancet*.
- Williams DR, Cooper LA. 2019. Structural racism and health. *JAMA*.
- National Academy of Medicine. 2021. *The Future of Nursing 2020-2030*.
- Shah MK et al. Nurse well-being and burnout synthesis papers.
- ANA and ICN workforce position papers remain essential professional references.

## 22.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Staffing, burnout, and faculty shortages
- Better care models for aging and chronic illness
- Expanded advanced-practice roles and scope variation
- Integration of genomics, digital monitoring, and AI into bedside care
- Stronger nursing-led intervention and implementation science
- More equitable workforce and patient outcomes
