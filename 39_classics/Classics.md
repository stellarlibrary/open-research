# 39. Classics

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Classics, Greek, Latin, Classical Studies, Ancient History, Classical Archaeology |
| Masters | MA in Classics, MA in Ancient History, MA in Classical Archaeology |
| PhD | Classics, Philology, Ancient History, Classical Archaeology |

**Note:** Strong programs still treat Greek and Latin language study as foundational, while many also integrate archaeology, reception, ancient philosophy, and digital classics.

---

## 39.1 Core Curriculum (Undergraduate)

### Core Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Latin Sequence** | Grammar, prose composition, close reading of prose and poetry |
| **Greek Sequence** | Attic Greek, Homeric Greek, translation, syntax, commentary use |
| **Greek & Roman Literature** | Epic, lyric, tragedy, comedy, historiography, rhetoric, philosophy |
| **Ancient History** | Archaic Greece through late Rome; institutions, warfare, empire, slavery, religion |
| **Classical Archaeology** | Art, architecture, urbanism, excavation reports, material culture |
| **Myth & Religion** | Greek myth, Roman religion, ritual, cult practice, reception |
| **Ancient Philosophy** | Plato, Aristotle, Hellenistic schools, late antique thought |

---

## 39.2 Graduate Specialization Areas

#### A. Greek & Latin Philology
- Textual criticism, commentary, lexicography, manuscript traditions, historical linguistics.

#### B. Ancient History
- Political, social, economic, legal, gender, and imperial histories of Greek and Roman worlds.

#### C. Classical Archaeology
- Sculpture, pottery, architecture, urbanism, burial, landscape, field methods.

#### D. Ancient Philosophy
- Plato, Aristotle, Stoics, Epicureans, Skeptics, Neoplatonism.

#### E. Reception & Classical Tradition
- Antiquity in modern literature, art, political thought, education, film, and digital media.

#### F. Late Antiquity / Early Christianity
- Christianization, empire, textuality, law, monasticism, Mediterranean transformation.

## 39.3 Key Methods & Tools

| Method/Tool | Application |
|---|---|
| **Philology** | Grammar, syntax, textual criticism, close reading in original languages |
| **Epigraphy** | Inscriptions for civic, legal, and commemorative history |
| **Papyrology** | Documentary and literary texts on papyrus |
| **Numismatics** | Coins as evidence for politics, economy, and iconography |
| **Archaeological Analysis** | Stratigraphy, typology, context, artifact interpretation |
| **Prosopography** | Reconstructing networks and biographies from fragmentary evidence |
| **Digital Classics** | TLG, Perseus, Pleiades, EAGLE, stylometry, GIS |

## 39.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **Digital recovery of damaged texts** - Imaging, machine learning, and papyrological reconstruction.
- **Race, empire, and field history** - Reassessing how Classics has been used in exclusionary political projects.
- **Ancient economy and inequality** - Stronger quantitative and comparative work on production, trade, and distribution.
- **Climate and disease in antiquity** - Linking textual, archaeological, and paleoclimate evidence.
- **Global reception** - Moving beyond Euro-American accounts of classical inheritance.
- **Materiality and everyday life** - More work on nonelite households, labor, gender, and provincial worlds.
