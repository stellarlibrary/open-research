# 27. Economics

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Economics, Mathematical Economics, Political Economy |
| Masters | MA/MS in Economics, Applied Economics, Quantitative Economics |
| PhD | Economics with field specializations |

**Note:** Economics is the most mathematically formal of the major social sciences. Undergraduate study emphasizes micro, macro, and econometrics; PhD study adds proof-based theory, advanced statistics, and field-defining empirical or theoretical research.

---

## 27.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Principles of microeconomics | Supply and demand, welfare, competition, externalities | Mankiw - *Principles of Economics* |
| Principles of macroeconomics | GDP, inflation, unemployment, fiscal and monetary policy | Mankiw - *Macroeconomics* |
| Intermediate microeconomics | Consumer and producer theory, game theory, GE, welfare | Varian - *Intermediate Microeconomics* |
| Intermediate macroeconomics | Growth, business cycles, policy, open economy basics | Blanchard - *Macroeconomics* |
| Econometrics | OLS, IV, panel data, causal designs | Wooldridge - *Introductory Econometrics* |
| Mathematical economics | Optimization, comparative statics, dynamic methods | Simon & Blume - *Mathematics for Economists* |

### Graduate depth
- Theory: MWG-style micro, dynamic macro, mechanism design, contract theory
- Econometrics: causal inference, panel methods, structural estimation, time series
- Fields: labor, development, public finance, IO, trade, finance, behavioral, political economy

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Microeconomics and welfare foundations
- Smith A. 1776. *The Wealth of Nations*.
- Ricardo D. 1817. *Principles of Political Economy and Taxation*.
- Marshall A. 1890. *Principles of Economics*.
- Arrow KJ. 1951. *Social Choice and Individual Values*.
- Debreu G. 1959. *Theory of Value*.
- Akerlof GA. 1970. "The market for lemons." *QJE*.

#### Macroeconomics and growth
- Keynes JM. 1936. *The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money*.
- Solow RM. 1956. Growth model. *QJE*.
- Friedman M. 1968. Role of monetary policy. *AER*.
- Lucas RE Jr. 1976. Critique. *Carnegie-Rochester*.
- Kydland FE, Prescott EC. 1982. Real business cycles. *Econometrica*.
- Clarida, Galí, Gertler. 1999. New Keynesian monetary policy. *JEL*.

#### Econometrics and identification
- Haavelmo T. 1944. Probability approach in econometrics.
- Rubin DB. 1974. Causal model. *J Educational Psychology*.
- Rosenbaum PR, Rubin DB. 1983. Propensity score. *Biometrika*.
- Card D, Krueger AB. 1994. Minimum wage and employment. *AER*.
- Angrist JD, Krueger AB. 1991. Returns to schooling via quarter of birth. *QJE*.
- Imbens G, Lemieux T. 2008. Regression discontinuity guide. *J Econometrics*.

#### Labor economics
- Becker GS. 1964. *Human Capital*.
- Mincer J. 1974. *Schooling, Experience, and Earnings*.
- Mortensen DT, Pissarides CA. 1994. Job creation and destruction. *REStud*.
- Autor DH, Katz LF, Kearney MS. 2008. Polarization of labor market. *AER Papers & Proceedings* / related papers.
- Card D. 1990. Mariel boatlift. *ILRR*.
- Chetty R et al. mobility atlas and intergenerational mobility papers.

#### Development economics
- Lewis WA. 1954. Dual-sector model. *Manchester School*.
- Sen A. 1981. *Poverty and Famines*.
- Banerjee AV, Duflo E. major RCT and development method papers.
- Kremer M. deworming and education papers.
- Acemoglu D, Johnson S, Robinson JA. 2001. Colonial origins. *AER*.
- Miguel E, Kremer M. 2004. Worms and schooling. *Econometrica*.

#### Industrial organization, trade, and finance
- Coase RH. 1937. Nature of the firm. *Economica*.
- Stigler GJ. 1961. Economics of information. *JPE*.
- Berry S, Levinsohn J, Pakes A. 1995. Automobile demand estimation. *Econometrica*.
- Krugman P. 1979. Increasing returns and trade. *JIE*.
- Melitz MJ. 2003. Trade with heterogeneous firms. *Econometrica*.
- Fama EF. 1970. Efficient capital markets. *Journal of Finance*.

#### Inequality, behavior, and public economics
- Kahneman D, Tversky A. 1979. Prospect theory. *Econometrica*.
- Mirrlees JA. 1971. Optimal income taxation. *REStud*.
- Piketty T, Saez E. 2003. Income inequality in the United States. *QJE*.
- Saez E. 2001. Optimal income transfer programs. *Econometrica*.
- Chetty R. 2009. Sufficient-statistics approach. *Annual Review of Economics*.
- Roth AE. 1984 and later. Matching and market design papers.

## 27.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- AI, productivity, and labor displacement
- Causal inference under complex interference and general equilibrium effects
- Climate damages, transition policy, and green industrial strategy
- Market power in digital and platform economies
- Inequality, wealth concentration, and tax design
- Better integration of behavioral, structural, and machine-learning methods
