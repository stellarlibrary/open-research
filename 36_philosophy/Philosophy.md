# 36. Philosophy

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Philosophy (BA), PPE, Logic, Philosophy of Science tracks |
| Masters | MA in Philosophy, MA in Logic, MA in Applied Ethics |
| PhD | Philosophy with area of specialization |

**Note:** Philosophy combines close reading, conceptual analysis, and formal argument. Most departments still organize around analytic, continental, history of philosophy, and applied or interdisciplinary areas.

---

## 36.1 Core Curriculum (Undergraduate)

### Core Philosophy Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Introduction to Philosophy** | Classic questions about knowledge, reality, freedom, morality, and meaning |
| **Logic** | Propositional and predicate logic, validity, proof, quantifiers, informal fallacies |
| **Ethics** | Utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics, applied ethics, metaethics |
| **Epistemology** | Skepticism, justification, Gettier, testimony, social epistemology |
| **Metaphysics** | Identity, causation, time, modality, free will, personhood |
| **Ancient Philosophy** | Plato, Aristotle, Hellenistic schools |
| **Modern Philosophy** | Descartes, Hume, Kant, early modern rationalism and empiricism |
| **Political Philosophy** | Authority, liberty, equality, justice, rights, democracy |

### Common Electives
- Philosophy of mind, language, science, law, religion, mathematics, feminism, race, aesthetics, phenomenology, existentialism, bioethics, environmental ethics.

## 36.2 Graduate Specialization Areas (AOS)

#### A. Metaphysics & Epistemology
- Modality, grounding, causation, personal identity, knowledge-first views, disagreement, epistemic normativity.

#### B. Mind, Language & Cognitive Science
- Consciousness, intentionality, perception, reference, semantics, pragmatics, AI and understanding.

#### C. Ethics & Political Philosophy
- Metaethics, moral psychology, responsibility, distributive justice, democratic theory, global justice.

#### D. Logic, Mathematics & Formal Philosophy
- Nonclassical logics, decision theory, formal epistemology, philosophy of math, proof and model theory.

#### E. History of Philosophy
- Ancient, medieval, early modern, Kantian, nineteenth-century, and twentieth-century traditions.

#### F. Continental & Critical Traditions
- Phenomenology, existentialism, hermeneutics, Frankfurt School, post-structuralism, feminist and race theory.

## 36.3 Key Methods

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Conceptual Analysis** | Clarifying necessary and sufficient conditions and testing counterexamples |
| **Formal Argumentation** | Symbolic logic, proofs, semantics, formal models |
| **Thought Experiments** | Using hypothetical cases to test intuitions and theories |
| **Textual Interpretation** | Close reading of canonical and contemporary texts |
| **Phenomenology / Hermeneutics** | Describing lived experience and interpretive structures |
| **Experimental / Empirical Philosophy** | Surveys, behavioral evidence, interdisciplinary data |
| **Normative Theory Construction** | Evaluating reasons, principles, and institutional designs |

## 36.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **AI, agency, and understanding** - Whether large models count as understanding, reasoning, or merely prediction; implications for ethics and epistemology.
- **Consciousness** - Competing physicalist, illusionist, panpsychist, and higher-order accounts remain unsettled.
- **Social epistemology** - Testimony, trust, misinformation, and epistemic injustice in networked publics.
- **Climate, risk, and intergenerational ethics** - Population ethics, longtermism, and duties to future persons.
- **Metaphysics after formalization** - Renewed work on grounding, dependence, modality, and structure.
- **Cross-tradition philosophy** - Greater integration of non-Western, feminist, and critical traditions into core curricula.
