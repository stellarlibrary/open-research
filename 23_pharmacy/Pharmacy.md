# 23. Pharmacy

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Pre-pharmacy | Biology, chemistry, organic chemistry, biochemistry, physiology, statistics |
| Professional degree | PharmD |
| Postgraduate training | PGY1, PGY2 residencies; fellowships |
| Graduate research | Pharmaceutical sciences, medicinal chemistry, PK/PD, pharmacogenomics, pharmaceutics |

**Note:** Pharmacy bridges chemistry, biomedical science, therapeutics, and health systems. The PharmD is practice-focused; research doctorates emphasize drug discovery, delivery, modeling, and outcomes.

## 23.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Pharmacotherapy | Disease-based medication management | DiPiro et al. - *Pharmacotherapy: A Pathophysiologic Approach* |
| Pharmacology | Mechanisms, receptors, adverse effects | Brunton et al. - *Goodman & Gilman's* |
| Biopharmaceutics/PK | ADME, dosing, drug levels, modeling | Shargel et al. - *Applied Biopharmaceutics & Pharmacokinetics* |
| Medicinal chemistry | SAR, target interaction, chemical optimization | Patrick - *An Introduction to Medicinal Chemistry* |
| Pharmaceutics | Dosage forms, compounding, delivery systems | Remington - *The Science and Practice of Pharmacy* |
| Dosage forms | Formulation design and drug delivery | Allen - *Ansel's Pharmaceutical Dosage Forms* |
| Clinical skills | Literature evaluation, patient counseling, MTM, immunization | Chisholm-Burns et al. - *Pharmacotherapy Principles and Practice* |

### Graduate depth
- Medicinal chemistry and target optimization
- PK/PD and model-informed precision dosing
- Drug delivery and biomaterials
- Pharmacogenomics and individualized therapy
- Social and administrative pharmacy, outcomes, and policy

| Pharmacogenomics | Weitzel et al. major teaching reviews plus CPIC resources |

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Drug action, receptors, and PK/PD
- Clark AJ. 1933. Receptor-occupancy concepts.
- Ariëns EJ, Stephenson RP, Black JW receptor-theory papers remain foundational.
- Rowland, Benet, and Graham clearance / disposition papers remain core PK readings.
- Wagner JG, Nelson E. 1964. Absorption and compartmental modeling methods.
- Sheiner LB, Beal SL. 1981–1983. Population PK/PD modeling papers.
- Holford NHG, Sheiner LB. 1981. PK/PD disease models. *Clinical Pharmacokinetics*.
- Levy G. 1994. Pharmacologic target-concentration strategy papers.

#### Clinical therapeutics landmarks
- CONSENSUS Trial Study Group. 1987. Enalapril in severe heart failure. *NEJM*.
- ISIS-2. 1988. Aspirin and thrombolysis after myocardial infarction. *Lancet*.
- SOLVD Investigators. 1991. ACE inhibition in LV dysfunction. *NEJM*.
- 4S Study. 1994. Simvastatin in CAD. *Lancet*.
- UKPDS Group. 1998. Intensive glucose control. *Lancet*.
- RALES Investigators. 1999. Spironolactone in severe heart failure. *NEJM*.
- ALLHAT. 2002. Antihypertensive comparative-effectiveness landmark.
- PARADIGM-HF. 2014. Sacubitril/valsartan in heart failure. *NEJM*.
- EMPA-REG OUTCOME. 2015. Cardiovascular benefit of empagliflozin. *NEJM*.

#### Infectious diseases and stewardship
- Fleming A. 1929. Penicillin paper. *British Journal of Experimental Pathology*.
- Craig WA. PK/PD of antimicrobials papers.
- Drusano GL. Antimicrobial exposure-response papers.
- Dellinger RP et al. Surviving Sepsis Campaign guideline series.
- Dellit TH et al. 2007. IDSA/SHEA antimicrobial-stewardship guidelines. *CID*.
- Barlam TF et al. 2016. Updated stewardship guidelines. *CID*.
- Tamma PD et al. Stewardship implementation reviews remain major teaching references.

#### Pharmacogenomics and precision medicine
- Evans WE, Relling MV. 1999. Pharmacogenomics in the clinic. *Science*.
- Mallal S et al. 2008. HLA-B*57:01 screening and abacavir hypersensitivity. *NEJM*.
- Relling MV, Evans WE. 2015. Pharmacogenomics in precision medicine. *Nature*.
- CPIC guideline papers for CYP2C19-clopidogrel, CYP2D6-codeine, TPMT-thiopurines.
- Johnson JA et al. Warfarin pharmacogenetics reviews.
- Dunnenberger HM et al. Implementation of preemptive pharmacogenomics. *Clinical Pharmacology & Therapeutics*.
- Caudle KE et al. Major CPIC process and implementation papers.

#### Pharmaceutics and delivery
- Higuchi T. 1961. Controlled release from matrices. *Journal of Pharmaceutical Sciences*.
- Korsmeyer RW, Gurny R, Doelker E, Buri P, Peppas NA. 1983. Release-mechanism modeling for polymeric systems.
- Amidon GL et al. 1995. Biopharmaceutics classification system. *Pharmaceutical Research*.
- Langer R. 1998. Drug delivery and biomaterials overview. *Nature*.
- Allen TM, Cullis PR. 2013. Liposomal drug delivery. *Science*.
- Kulkarni JA et al. 2018. Lipid-nanoparticle formulation review. *Nature Nanotechnology*.
- Hou X et al. mRNA LNP delivery reviews.

#### Practice, adherence, and outcomes
- Hepler CD, Strand LM. 1990. Pharmaceutical care. *AJHP*.
- Cipolle, Strand, Morley medication-therapy-management framework papers.
- Kaboli PJ et al. 2006. Clinical pharmacist interventions systematic review. *Archives of Internal Medicine*.
- Haynes RB et al. Adherence-intervention reviews.
- Chisholm-Burns MA et al. 2010. Pharmacist effects on outcomes meta-analysis. *Medical Care*.
- Bond CA, Raehl CL. Pharmacist staffing/outcome studies.
- ASHP and ACCP guideline statements remain core professional-practice references.

## 23.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- More reliable precision dosing at point of care
- Drug shortages and resilient supply chains
- Better oral delivery for biologics and RNA therapeutics
- Real-world comparative effectiveness and safety
- Expansion of pharmacist scope with strong outcome evidence
- Better stewardship, deprescribing, and adherence support
