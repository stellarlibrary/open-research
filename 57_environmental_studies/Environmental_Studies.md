# 57. Environmental Studies & Sustainability

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Environmental Studies, Sustainability, Environmental Policy |
| Masters | MEM, MESc, MS in Sustainability, environmental policy and management degrees |
| PhD | Environmental Studies, Sustainability Science, Environment and Resources |

**Note:** Environmental Studies is the interdisciplinary counterpart to more natural-science-heavy environmental science. Programs are built around coupled human-natural systems, governance, justice, and sustainability transitions.

---

## 57.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Intro environmental studies | Human-environment systems, sustainability, justice | Cunningham & Cunningham - *Environmental Science: A Global Concern* |
| Environmental science | Ecology, hydrology, climate, biogeochemistry, pollution | Miller & Spoolman or equivalent environmental science texts |
| Environmental policy and law | US regulation, international agreements, governance | Kraft - *Environmental Policy and Politics* |
| Environmental economics | Externalities, valuation, carbon pricing, natural capital | Tietenberg & Lewis - *Environmental and Natural Resource Economics* |
| Sustainability science | Resilience, SDGs, transitions, systems thinking | Clark, Kates and related core sustainability literature |
| Climate change | Science, mitigation, adaptation, energy transition | IPCC assessment reports |
| Conservation and biodiversity | Protected areas, restoration, extinction drivers | Primack - *Essentials of Conservation Biology* |
| Environmental justice | Racism, exposure, rights, participation, Indigenous governance | Bullard - *Dumping in Dixie* |

### Graduate depth
- Climate and energy transitions
- Conservation and biodiversity governance
- Environmental justice and political ecology
- Sustainability science and social-ecological systems
- Water, food, and urban sustainability
- Environmental humanities and ethics

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Foundational environmental thought
- Marsh GP. 1864. *Man and Nature*.
- Leopold A. 1949. *A Sand County Almanac*.
- Carson R. 1962. *Silent Spring*.
- Hardin G. 1968. tragedy of the commons. *Science*.
- Meadows DH et al. 1972. *The Limits to Growth*.
- Brundtland Commission. 1987. *Our Common Future*.

#### Sustainability science and social-ecological systems
- Kates RW et al. 2001. sustainability science. *Science*.
- Ostrom E. 2009. social-ecological systems framework. *Science*.
- Folke C. 2006. resilience perspective. *Global Environmental Change*.
- Cash DW et al. 2003. scale and cross-scale dynamics. *PNAS*.
- Clark WC, Harley AG. 2020. sustainability science overview. *PNAS*.
- Raworth K. doughnut economics framing writings.

#### Climate change, earth systems, and boundaries
- Keeling CD. atmospheric CO2 measurement series.
- Arrhenius S. greenhouse-effect precursor calculations.
- IPCC Assessment Reports. major synthesis baseline.
- Rockström J et al. 2009. planetary boundaries. *Nature*.
- Steffen W et al. 2015. updated planetary boundaries. *Science*.
- IPBES global assessment. biodiversity-system synthesis.

#### Environmental justice and political ecology
- Bullard RD. environmental racism and justice writings.
- Mohai P, Pellow D, Roberts JT. environmental justice review. *Annual Review*.
- Schlosberg D. 2007. *Defining Environmental Justice*.
- Whyte KP. Indigenous climate justice papers.
- Pulido L. race and environmental inequality studies.
- Martinez-Alier J. ecological distribution conflicts writings.

#### Conservation, biodiversity, and restoration
- MacArthur RH, Wilson EO. 1967. *The Theory of Island Biogeography*.
- Soulé ME. 1985. what is conservation biology? *BioScience*.
- Myers N et al. 2000. biodiversity hotspots. *Nature*.
- Halpern BS et al. cumulative human impacts on marine ecosystems. *Science*.
- Mace GM. biodiversity metrics and conservation priorities papers.
- Seddon N et al. nature-based solutions reviews. *Philosophical Transactions B*.

#### Energy, transitions, and policy
- Stern N. 2006. *Stern Review*.
- Pacala S, Socolow R. 2004. stabilization wedges. *Science*.
- Jacobson MZ, Delucchi MA. renewable energy pathways papers.
- Sovacool BK. energy transitions and justice studies.
- Geels FW. multi-level perspective on transitions. *Research Policy*.
- Jenkins K et al. energy justice framework papers.

#### Food, water, and urban sustainability
- Falkenmark M. water stress concepts.
- Rockström J. water-food-climate nexus papers.
- Tilman D, Clark M. diets and sustainability papers. *Nature*.
- Foley JA et al. global land-use and food-system synthesis. *Nature*.
- Seto KC et al. urban expansion and biodiversity/climate impacts. *PNAS*.
- Elmqvist T et al. urban resilience and green infrastructure writings.

## 57.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Turning climate targets into politically durable transitions
- Measuring justice and equity in adaptation and decarbonization
- Biodiversity protection under land, food, and energy pressure
- Better operationalization of planetary boundaries
- Avoiding greenwashing in ESG, carbon markets, and nature-based solutions
- Linking local participatory work with global-scale earth-system modeling
