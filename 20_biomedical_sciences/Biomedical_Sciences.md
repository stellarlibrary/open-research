# 20. Biomedical Sciences (PhD Track)

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate preparation | Biology, biochemistry, chemistry, biomedical sciences, neuroscience |
| Masters | MS in Biomedical Sciences, Pharmacology, Physiology, Molecular Medicine |
| PhD | Integrated Biomedical Sciences, Immunology, Pathology, Pharmacology, Cancer Biology, Microbiology, Toxicology |
| Combined | MD/PhD, MSTP |

**Note:** Biomedical sciences sit at the research core of the medical school ecosystem. Training emphasizes mechanism, experimentation, quantitative analysis, and translation from molecule to organism to therapy.

---

## 20.1 Core Curriculum

### Typical entry preparation
- General biology, chemistry, organic chemistry, biochemistry, physics
- Cell biology, molecular biology, genetics, statistics
- At least one substantial research experience

### First-year graduate core

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Molecular and cell biology | Replication, transcription, signaling, trafficking, cell cycle | Alberts et al. - *Molecular Biology of the Cell* |
| Biochemistry | Protein structure, metabolism, enzymology | Nelson & Cox - *Lehninger Principles of Biochemistry* |
| Genetics and genomics | Gene regulation, inheritance, CRISPR, sequencing | Watson et al. - *Molecular Biology of the Gene* |
| Immunology | Innate/adaptive immunity, tolerance, inflammation | Murphy & Weaver - *Janeway's Immunobiology* |
| Physiology/pathophysiology | Organ systems and disease mechanisms | Hall - *Guyton and Hall*; Robbins & Cotran |
| Statistics and experimental design | Inference, power, controls, reproducibility | Motulsky - *Intuitive Biostatistics* |

### Main graduate tracks
- Pharmacology
- Physiology
- Immunology
- Cancer biology
- Infectious disease biology
- Molecular medicine
- Toxicology

---

## 20.2 Graduate Depth

| Area | Core research questions |
|---|---|
| Cell signaling | How cells sense, compute, and respond to cues |
| Gene regulation | How chromatin, transcription factors, and RNA shape phenotype |
| Host-pathogen interaction | How microbes invade, persist, and evade immunity |
| Immunology | How immune systems protect, misfire, or can be reprogrammed |
| Cancer biology | How tumors evolve, metastasize, and resist therapy |
| Translational science | How biomarkers, models, and interventions move toward the clinic |

### Standard tools of training
- Lab rotations
- Journal clubs
- Qualifying exam or grant-style proposal
- Reproducible data analysis
- Scientific writing and peer review

## 20.3 Key Methods & Tools

| Domain | Core methods |
|---|---|

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Molecular biology and gene regulation
- Watson JD, Crick FHC. 1953. DNA structure papers. *Nature*.
- Jacob F, Monod J. 1961. Genetic regulatory mechanisms. *Journal of Molecular Biology*.
- Monod J, Wyman J, Changeux JP. 1965. Allosteric transitions. *JMB*.
- Baltimore D. 1970. Reverse transcriptase. *Nature*.
- Sharp PA, Roberts RJ. 1977. RNA splicing discovery papers. *Cell*.
- Fire A et al. 1998. RNA interference. *Nature*.

#### Cell signaling and cell biology
- Palade GE. 1975. Intracellular aspects of protein synthesis. Nobel-era secretory pathway work.
- Brown MS, Goldstein JL. 1986. LDL receptor and receptor-mediated endocytosis. *Science*.
- Hartwell LH et al. 1997. Cell cycle control synthesis papers.
- Hanahan D, Weinberg RA. 2000. "The hallmarks of cancer." *Cell*.
- Hanahan D. 2022. "Hallmarks of cancer: new dimensions." *Cancer Discovery*.
- Alberts B et al. classic checkpoint and chromosome papers remain central journal-club staples.

#### Immunology
- Burnet FM. 1957. Clonal selection theory.
- Zinkernagel RM, Doherty PC. 1974. MHC restriction. *Nature*.
- Mosmann TR et al. 1986. TH1/TH2 paradigm. *J Immunology*.
- Matzinger P. 1994. Danger model. *Annual Review of Immunology*.
- Iwasaki A, Medzhitov R. 2015. Innate immunity control of adaptive responses. *Nature Immunology*.
- June CH et al. 2018. CAR-T review. *Science*.

#### Cancer biology and genomics
- Knudson AG. 1971. Two-hit hypothesis. *PNAS*.
- Vogelstein B et al. 1988. APC and colorectal tumor progression papers.
- Fearon ER, Vogelstein B. 1990. Genetic model for colorectal tumorigenesis. *Cell*.
- TCGA Pan-Cancer analyses. 2013-2018. Multi-tumor genomics reference set.
- Sharma SV, Lee DY, Li B et al. 2010. Drug-tolerant persisters. *Cell*.
- Jamal-Hanjani M et al. 2017. TRACERx and intratumor evolution. *NEJM*.

#### Infectious disease and microbiology
- Avery OT, MacLeod CM, McCarty M. 1944. DNA as transforming principle. *J Exp Med*.
- Lederberg J, Tatum EL. 1946. Bacterial conjugation. *Nature*.
- Woese CR, Fox GE. 1977. Discovery of Archaea. *PNAS*.
- Marshall BJ, Warren JR. 1984. *H. pylori* and gastritis. *Lancet*.
- Doudna JA, Charpentier E. 2014. CRISPR-Cas9 review. *Science*.
- Andersen KG et al. 2020. SARS-CoV-2 proximal origin. *Nature Medicine*.

#### Translational medicine and methods
- Friedman LM, Furberg C, DeMets D. Clinical trials framework texts and related methodological papers remain standard.
- Ioannidis JPA. 2005. "Why most published research findings are false." *PLoS Medicine*.
- Begley CG, Ellis LM. 2012. Raise standards for preclinical cancer research. *Nature*.
- Collins FS. 2011. Reengineering translational science. *Science Translational Medicine*.
- Shah SP et al. 2012 onward. Single-cell and tumor heterogeneity method papers.
- Trapnell C et al. 2014. Pseudotime/single-cell trajectory inference. *Nature Biotechnology*.

## 20.5 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Causal interpretation of high-dimensional omics
- Better disease models beyond standard cell lines
- Immune engineering for cancer and autoimmunity
- Gene editing delivery, specificity, and durability
- Mechanisms of resistance, relapse, and persistence
- Reproducibility, assay standardization, and data integration
