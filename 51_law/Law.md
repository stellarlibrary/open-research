# 51. Law

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Professional | JD, LLB (outside US) |
| Advanced | LLM, JSD/SJD, PhD in Law |
| Joint | JD/MBA, JD/MPP, JD/PhD, JD/MA |

**Note:** Law is an intensive professional curriculum organized around case analysis, statutory interpretation, doctrinal synthesis, and increasingly clinical and interdisciplinary training.

---

## 51.1 JD Curriculum

### Required First-Year (1L) Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Contracts** | Formation, breach, remedies, interpretation, UCC basics |
| **Torts** | Negligence, intentional torts, products liability, causation, damages |
| **Civil Procedure** | Jurisdiction, pleadings, discovery, summary judgment, appeal |
| **Criminal Law** | Elements, defenses, homicide, inchoate crimes, punishment |
| **Constitutional Law** | Judicial review, federalism, separation of powers, rights |
| **Property** | Estates, land use, takings, landlord-tenant, servitudes |
| **Legal Research & Writing** | Briefs, memos, statutory interpretation, citation, oral argument |

### Upper-Level (2L-3L) — Electives + Experiential
- Evidence, Professional Responsibility, Administrative Law, Business Associations, Tax, Family Law, Environmental Law, IP, Labor & Employment, Clinics, externships, moot court, journals.

---

## 51.2 Specialization Areas (LLM / Practice Areas)

#### A. Corporate / Business Law
- M&A, securities, finance, governance, private equity, restructuring.

#### B. Constitutional / Public Law
- Rights, federal courts, administrative state, election law, civil rights.

#### C. Criminal Law & Procedure
- Investigation, defense, punishment, policing, sentencing, evidence.

#### D. Intellectual Property & Technology Law
- Copyright, trademark, patent, privacy, AI, platform governance.

#### E. International / Comparative Law
- Treaties, trade, human rights, migration, conflict, comparative institutions.

#### F. Environmental, Health & Bio Law
- Climate, energy, regulation, FDA, public health, biotechnology.

---

## 51.3 Key Methods & Sources

| Method | Use |
|---|---|
| **Case Method** | Reading appellate opinions, extracting rules, analogizing facts |
| **Statutory Interpretation** | Text, structure, history, purpose, canons |
| **Doctrinal Synthesis** | Turning many cases and statutes into coherent legal frameworks |
| **Clinical / Experiential Practice** | Interviewing, counseling, drafting, advocacy, negotiation |
| **Empirical Legal Studies** | Quantitative and qualitative study of legal institutions and outcomes |
| **Comparative / Interdisciplinary Analysis** | Economics, philosophy, sociology, and history in legal argument |

## 51.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **AI and legal judgment** - Automation in research, drafting, adjudication, and discovery.
- **Administrative state and constitutional conflict** - Delegation, deference, and democratic legitimacy.
- **Climate and environmental litigation** - Corporate liability, adaptation, and intergenerational claims.
- **Platform governance and privacy** - Speech, surveillance, competition, and cross-border regulation.
- **Access to justice** - Cost, representation gaps, legal deserts, and procedural reform.
- **Criminal justice reform** - Sentencing, police accountability, data systems, and decarceration.
