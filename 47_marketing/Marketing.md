## 47.1 Core Curriculum

### Core Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principles of Marketing** | STP, 4 Ps, customer value, competition, channels |
| **Consumer Behavior** | Motivation, identity, choice, social influence, behavioral bias |
| **Marketing Research** | Survey design, experiments, conjoint, segmentation, inference |
| **Brand Management** | Positioning, equity, architecture, communication |
| **Digital Marketing** | Search, social, CRM, content, attribution, analytics |
| **Marketing Strategy** | Growth, portfolio choices, channel design, retention, pricing |
| **Sales / B2B / Analytics electives** | Pipeline management, account-based marketing, dashboards, CLV |

---

## 47.2 Graduate Specialization Areas (PhD)

#### A. Quantitative Marketing
- Demand estimation, choice models, targeting, dynamic pricing, experimentation.

#### B. Consumer Behavior
- Judgment and decision-making, identity, emotion, culture, persuasion.

#### C. Strategy and Channels
- Innovation, platform ecosystems, competition, retailer-manufacturer relations.

#### D. Digital / Social Media Marketing
- Influencers, recommendation systems, privacy, attribution, creator economies.

| Method | Application |

## 47.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **Generative AI and personalization** - Creative automation, targeting, and authenticity.
- **Privacy-constrained measurement** - Attribution after cookies and mobile identifier restrictions.
- **Platform and creator economies** - Dependence on algorithms, influencers, and marketplace rules.
- **Sustainability and green claims** - Trust, regulation, and consumer response to environmental messaging.
- **Omnichannel experience** - Integrating retail, e-commerce, service, and community touchpoints.
- **Causal analytics** - Better experimentation and incrementality in noisy media environments.
