# 32. Linguistics

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Linguistics, Applied Linguistics, Cognitive Science tracks |
| Masters | Linguistics, Applied Linguistics, TESOL, Computational Linguistics |
| PhD | Linguistics and subfield specializations |

**Note:** Linguistics is the scientific study of language structure, meaning, use, acquisition, and change. It spans formal theory, laboratory work, field documentation, and computation.

---

## 32.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Intro linguistics | Phonetics, phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, pragmatics | Fromkin, Rodman & Hyams - *An Introduction to Language* |
| Phonetics | IPA, articulation, acoustics, speech analysis | Ladefoged & Johnson - *A Course in Phonetics* |
| Phonology | Features, phonological rules, metrical/prosodic systems | Hayes - *Introductory Phonology* |
| Morphology | Word formation, inflection, typology | Lieber - *Introducing Morphology* |
| Syntax | Phrase structure, movement, agreement, argument structure | Carnie - *Syntax* |
| Semantics | Compositionality, truth conditions, quantification | Heim & Kratzer - *Semantics in Generative Grammar* |
| Pragmatics | Implicature, deixis, speech acts, discourse | Levinson - *Pragmatics* |
| Historical linguistics | Sound change, reconstruction, language families | Campbell - *Historical Linguistics* |
| Sociolinguistics | Variation, identity, language contact, ideology | Wardhaugh & Fuller - *An Introduction to Sociolinguistics* |

### Graduate depth
- Formal syntax, semantics, and phonology
- Psycholinguistics and neurolinguistics
- Language acquisition and bilingualism
- Sociolinguistics and discourse
- Documentation, typology, and historical linguistics
- Computational linguistics and NLP

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Foundational structural and generative theory
- Saussure F de. *Course in General Linguistics*.
- Bloomfield L. 1933. *Language*.
- Chomsky N. 1957. *Syntactic Structures*.
- Chomsky N. 1965. *Aspects of the Theory of Syntax*.
- Chomsky N. 1981. *Lectures on Government and Binding*.
- Chomsky N. 1995. *The Minimalist Program*.

#### Phonetics and phonology
- Jakobson R, Fant G, Halle M. distinctive feature work.
- Chomsky N, Halle M. 1968. *The Sound Pattern of English*.
- Labov W, Yaeger M, Steiner R. quantitative phonology and variation studies.
- Pierrehumbert J. intonational phonology papers.
- Prince A, Smolensky P. Optimality Theory manuscript.
- Hayes B and later metrical phonology/prosody papers.

#### Syntax and formal semantics
- Ross JR. 1967. constraints on variables in syntax.
- Bresnan J. lexical-functional grammar papers.
- Gazdar G et al. generalized phrase structure grammar work.
- Montague R. 1970-1974. formal semantics papers.
- Heim I. 1982. file change semantics dissertation.
- Kratzer A. event semantics and modality papers.

#### Pragmatics and discourse
- Austin JL. 1962. *How to Do Things with Words*.
- Grice HP. 1975. logic and conversation. *Syntax and Semantics*.
- Searle JR. speech act writings.
- Sperber D, Wilson D. relevance theory work.
- Brown P, Levinson SC. politeness theory.
- Clark HH. common ground and language use papers.

#### Acquisition and psycholinguistics
- Brown R. 1973. *A First Language*.
- Newport EL. less is more and critical period work.
- Fodor JA, Bever TG, Garrett MF. sentence processing classics.
- Tanenhaus MK et al. 1995. visual world paradigm. *Science*.
- MacDonald MC, Pearlmutter NJ, Seidenberg MS. constraint-based parsing. *Psychological Review*.
- Kutas M, Hillyard SA. 1980. N400 paper. *Science*.

#### Variation, society, and language change
- Labov W. 1963. Martha's Vineyard. *Word*.
- Labov W. 1966. *The Social Stratification of English in New York City*.
- Weinreich U, Labov W, Herzog M. 1968. empirical foundations for language change.
- Eckert P. 2000. *Linguistic Variation as Social Practice*.
- Silverstein M. language ideology papers.
- Bucholtz M, Hall K. identity and interaction papers. *Discourse Studies*.

#### Historical linguistics, typology, and documentation
- Grimm J. Grimm's Law foundational work.
- Greenberg JH. 1963. universals of grammar. *Universals of Language*.
- Hopper P, Traugott E. grammaticalization theory writings.
- Dixon RMW. language diversity and areal typology works.
- Himmelmann N. language documentation framework papers.
- Hale K et al. 1992. endangered languages and linguistic research. *Language*.

#### Computational linguistics and current frontiers
- Shannon CE. 1948. information theory. *Bell System Technical Journal*.
- Jurafsky and Martin-related statistical NLP syntheses remain core curricular anchors.
- Mikolov T et al. 2013. word2vec papers.
- Vaswani A et al. 2017. Transformers. *NeurIPS*.
- Linzen T, Dupoux E, Goldberg Y. 2016. syntax sensitivity in neural models. *TACL*.
- Bender EM, Koller A. 2020. meaning and form in NLP. *ACL*.

## 32.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- What LLMs do and do not learn about syntax and meaning
- Better theory-method integration across formal and usage-based camps
- Documentation and revitalization for endangered languages
- Stronger cross-linguistic sampling beyond major written languages
- Multimodal and sign-language linguistics at scale
- More realistic psycholinguistic models of dialogue and interaction
