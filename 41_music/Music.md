## 41.1 Core Curriculum (BM)

### Core Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Music Theory I-IV** | Tonal harmony, form, counterpoint, chromaticism, post-tonal basics |
| **Aural Skills** | Sight-singing, dictation, rhythm, keyboard harmony |
| **Music History** | Medieval through contemporary repertories and contexts |
| **Applied Lessons** | Weekly studio instruction, juries, recitals |
| **Ensembles** | Orchestra, choir, wind, chamber, jazz, or contemporary ensembles |
| **Keyboard / Conducting** | Piano proficiency, score reading, rehearsal technique |
| **Specialization Courses** | Performance, composition, technology, education, musicology tracks |

### Specialization Tracks
- **Performance** - Repertory, technique, pedagogy, recital preparation.
- **Composition** - Counterpoint, orchestration, notation, electronic media, portfolio.
- **Music Education** - Methods, conducting, classroom teaching, certification.
- **Music Technology** - Recording, mixing, acoustics, DAWs, interactive media.

---

## 41.2 Graduate Specialization Areas

#### A. Musicology
- Historical study of repertories, institutions, media, reception, opera, popular music, sound studies.

#### B. Ethnomusicology
- Fieldwork, diaspora, performance, Indigenous and regional traditions, decolonial methods.

#### C. Music Theory
- Schenkerian analysis, post-tonal theory, rhythm and meter, form, popular music theory.

#### D. Composition & Creative Practice
- Acoustic, electroacoustic, film/game scoring, improvisation, multimedia work.

#### E. Music Cognition / Education
- Perception, development, pedagogy, assessment, music and wellbeing.

## 41.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **AI and composition** - Creative assistance, ownership, style modeling, and pedagogy.
- **Streaming and labor** - Platform economics, metadata, recommendation systems, and compensation.
- **Decolonizing curricula** - Rebalancing Western art music with global, popular, and Indigenous traditions.
- **Music, health, and cognition** - Better causal evidence on therapy, learning, and neural plasticity.
- **Sound studies and media** - Expanding music scholarship into listening environments and infrastructures.
- **Preservation of endangered traditions** - Community-led archives, rights, and circulation ethics.
