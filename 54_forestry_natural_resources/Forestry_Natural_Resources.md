## 54.1 Core Curriculum

### Major Sub-disciplines

| Area | Content |
|---|---|
| **Silviculture** | Stand dynamics, regeneration, thinning, fire, uneven/even-aged management |
| **Forest Ecology** | Succession, disturbance, soils, nutrient cycles, landscape ecology |
| **Mensuration & Biometrics** | Measurement, inventory, growth and yield, sampling, lidar |
| **Wildlife Ecology** | Habitat, population dynamics, monitoring, conservation, conflict management |
| **Fisheries Science** | Aquatic ecology, stock assessment, restoration, aquaculture |
| **Forest Policy & Economics** | Public lands, markets, rotations, certification, governance |
| **Fire Science / Wood Science** | Fire behavior, fuels, wildfire management, timber and wood products |

---

## 54.2 Key Methods & Tools

| Method | Application |
|---|---|
| **Field Inventory** | Tree measurements, stand assessment, plot sampling |
| **Remote Sensing / GIS** | Canopy mapping, habitat analysis, fire and land-cover monitoring |
| **Wildlife / Fisheries Monitoring** | Mark-recapture, eDNA, telemetry, stock assessment |
| **Silvicultural Experimentation** | Treatment effects, regeneration trials, fuel reduction studies |
| **Economic / Policy Analysis** | Rotation models, ecosystem services, public lands tradeoffs |
| **Landscape Modeling** | Fire spread, connectivity, climate adaptation, watershed planning |

## 54.4 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **Wildfire and smoke** - Managing fuels, ecosystems, and communities under hotter climates.
- **Forest carbon and offsets** - Measurement, permanence, leakage, and justice concerns.
- **Biodiversity decline** - Connectivity, assisted migration, and working-landscape conservation.
- **Invasive species and forest health** - Climate-linked outbreaks and ecosystem transformation.
- **Remote sensing and eDNA** - Better monitoring across large and inaccessible landscapes.
- **Indigenous stewardship and governance** - Shared management and decolonizing conservation policy.
