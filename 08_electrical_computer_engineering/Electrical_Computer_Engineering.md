# 8. Electrical & Computer Engineering

## Degree Programs

| Level | Common programs | What changes by level |
|---|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, combined ECE programs | Builds circuits, electronics, signals, computation, electromagnetics, and embedded systems |
| Master's | MSEE, MS in Computer Engineering, MEng in ECE, specialized MS in power, signal processing, communications, VLSI, robotics | Adds technical depth and systems specialization |
| Doctoral | PhD in Electrical Engineering or Computer Engineering with subfields such as devices, circuits, systems, power, controls, communications, or hardware-software systems | Focuses on original research, lab infrastructure, and dissertation work |

ECE sits at the junction of **physics, mathematics, hardware, and computation**. Electrical engineering emphasizes circuits, fields, signals, power, and devices; computer engineering emphasizes digital hardware, architecture, embedded systems, and the hardware-software boundary.

---

## 8.1 Core Curriculum (Undergraduate)

Strong ECE training moves from mathematical and physical foundations into analog circuits, digital systems, signals, communication, computing hardware, and design labs.

### Foundation sequence

| Course | Typical content | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Calculus, differential equations, linear algebra** | Continuous and discrete math, transforms, eigenproblems | Core language for circuits, signals, electromagnetics, and controls |
| **Physics and chemistry** | E&M, waves, modern physics, basic materials chemistry | Supports semiconductor devices, fields, and energy systems |
| **Programming** | C/C++, Python, data structures, software for hardware systems | Essential for embedded systems, simulation, and data workflows |
| **Discrete mathematics** | Logic, combinatorics, Boolean algebra | Important for digital design and computer architecture |
| **Probability and random processes** | Noise, random variables, stochastic processes, estimation basics | Central for communications, DSP, and control |

### Core upper-division areas

| Area | Typical courses | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Circuits and electronics** | Circuit Analysis, Electronics, Analog Design | Builds the analog foundation of devices and systems |
| **Digital systems and architecture** | Digital Logic, Computer Architecture, Embedded Systems | Connects hardware design to computation |
| **Signals, systems, and DSP** | Signals and Systems, DSP, estimation | Core framework for filtering, audio, imaging, and communications |
| **Communications and information** | Communication Systems, coding, wireless basics | Teaches how information is represented and transmitted |
| **Electromagnetics and RF** | Fields, transmission lines, waves, antennas, microwave engineering | Needed for wireless, RFIC, radar, photonics, and power |
| **Controls and power** | Control Systems, electric machines, power electronics, power systems | Important for automation, robotics, energy, and infrastructure |

### Laboratory and design expectations

- **Circuits and electronics labs**: breadboarding, oscilloscopes, op-amps, transistor characterization
- **Digital and FPGA labs**: Verilog/VHDL, logic analyzers, FPGA prototyping
- **Embedded systems labs**: microcontrollers, RTOS, sensor integration, motor control
- **Communications and DSP labs**: SDRs, MATLAB/Simulink, spectral analysis, coding experiments
- **Capstone design**: hardware-software systems, robotics, chip design, communication systems, or power prototypes

## 8.2 Graduate Depth (MS & PhD)

Graduate ECE diverges into specialized research tracks with different toolchains, facilities, and publication cultures. The key shift is from learning standard architectures and methods to inventing new devices, algorithms, circuits, or systems.

### Typical first-year graduate focus

| Area | Typical graduate emphasis | Common milestones |
|---|---|---|
| **Signal processing / ML** | Statistical estimation, optimization, adaptive filtering, deep learning, inverse problems | Theory courses plus coding- and dataset-heavy projects |
| **VLSI / devices** | CMOS design, semiconductor devices, fabrication constraints, layout, verification | Tapeout-oriented work or device lab work |
| **Power and energy** | Stability, converters, optimization, smart grids, storage, protection | Simulation and hardware validation |
| **Communications / networking** | Information theory, coding, wireless systems, optical links, network optimization | Analytical and simulation-heavy milestone exams |
| **Controls / robotics** | Optimal, robust, nonlinear, networked, and learning-based control | Lab integration, proofs, and systems demonstrations |
| **Architecture / embedded / hardware systems** | Accelerators, security, RISC-V, heterogeneous systems, low-power design | FPGA/ASIC and software co-design |

### Typical PhD structure

- **Advanced coursework and qualifying exams**
- **Lab or advisor selection** in circuits, devices, power, signals, robotics, or systems groups
- **Use of specialized infrastructure** such as cleanrooms, PCB/FPGA labs, RF chambers, power labs, or robotics labs
- **Conference-driven publication culture** in some subfields and journal-driven culture in others
- **Dissertation** based on original design, theory, algorithm, system, or device contributions

### Major research tracks

#### Signal processing and machine learning
- Statistical signal processing, adaptive filters, image/speech processing, biomedical signals, radar, sensor fusion, graph signals, learning-based inference

#### VLSI, semiconductors, and devices
- Analog/mixed-signal ICs, digital VLSI, memory, semiconductor physics, photonics, RFICs, fabrication-aware architecture, wide-bandgap devices

#### Power and energy systems
- Power electronics, smart grid, renewable integration, storage, EV charging, converters, grid resilience, power-market modeling

#### Communications and networking
- Wireless, 5G/6G, coding, optical communications, information theory, IoT networking, network optimization, quantum communication

#### Controls, autonomy, and robotics
- Estimation, MPC, nonlinear control, distributed control, autonomous systems, cyber-physical systems, learning-enabled control

#### Computer engineering and hardware systems
- Computer architecture, embedded systems, accelerators, security, reconfigurable computing, edge AI, hardware-software codesign

## 8.3 Key Methods, Tools, and Research Infrastructure

### Software and design tools

| Tool family | Examples | Common role |
|---|---|---|
| **Numerical and signal tools** | MATLAB, Simulink, Python, NumPy, SciPy, PyTorch | Analysis, control, ML, DSP, simulation |

| **Power-system simulation** | PSCAD, PowerWorld, Simscape Electrical | Grid and converter analysis |
| **Embedded toolchains** | GCC, Keil, JTAG debuggers, RTOS tools | Firmware and low-level systems work |

### Experimental infrastructure

| Facility / method | Typical role |
|---|---|
| **Oscilloscopes, VNAs, spectrum analyzers, logic analyzers** | Core measurement for circuits, RF, and digital systems |
| **Anechoic chambers and antenna ranges** | RF and wireless testing |
| **Power electronics and machines labs** | Converter, inverter, and grid-integration experiments |

## 8.4 Foundational Texts and Learning Resources

ECE needs course-by-course, level-aware guidance because broad undergraduate texts, mathematically rigorous graduate texts, and design references play very different roles.

| Course / stage | Introductory or accessible | Standard major text | Advanced / graduate continuation | Why this set matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Circuit analysis** | Nilsson and Riedel, *Electric Circuits* | Hayt, Kemmerly, and Durbin, *Engineering Circuit Analysis* | Desoer and Kuh, *Basic Circuit Theory* | Standard foundation for EE analysis |
| **Electronics / microelectronics** | Razavi, *Fundamentals of Microelectronics* | Sedra and Smith, *Microelectronic Circuits* | Razavi, *Design of Analog CMOS Integrated Circuits* | Standard progression from first analog course to graduate IC design |
| **Digital logic and computer architecture** | Mano and Ciletti, *Digital Design* | Harris and Harris, *Digital Design and Computer Architecture* | Patterson and Hennessy, *Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach* | Core CpE route from logic to serious systems design |
| **Signals and systems** | Lathi, *Linear Systems and Signals* | Oppenheim and Willsky, *Signals and Systems* | Oppenheim and Schafer, *Discrete-Time Signal Processing* | Canonical signal-processing foundation |
| **Probability and random processes** | Yates and Goodman, *Probability and Stochastic Processes* | Papoulis and Pillai, *Probability, Random Variables, and Stochastic Processes* | Kay, *Fundamentals of Statistical Signal Processing* | Central for communications, DSP, estimation |
| **Communications** | Haykin, *Communication Systems* | Proakis and Salehi, *Digital Communications* | Tse and Viswanath, *Fundamentals of Wireless Communication*; Cover and Thomas, *Elements of Information Theory* | Standard route from first communication course to modern wireless/information theory |
| **Electromagnetics / RF** | Griffiths, *Introduction to Electrodynamics* | Hayt and Buck, *Engineering Electromagnetics* | Pozar, *Microwave Engineering*; Balanis, *Antenna Theory* | Core progression from field theory to microwave and antenna design |
| **Controls** | Nise, *Control Systems Engineering* | Ogata, *Modern Control Engineering* | Åström and Murray, *Feedback Systems*; Zhou, Doyle, and Glover, *Robust and Optimal Control* | Standard undergraduate-to-graduate controls path |
| **Power systems / power electronics** | Glover, Sarma, and Overbye, *Power System Analysis and Design* | Glover, Sarma, and Overbye, *Power System Analysis and Design* | Kundur, *Power System Stability and Control*; Erickson and Maksimović, *Fundamentals of Power Electronics* | Canonical route into modern energy systems |
| **Semiconductor devices** | Neamen, *Semiconductor Physics and Devices* | Neamen, *Semiconductor Physics and Devices* | Sze and Ng, *Physics of Semiconductor Devices* | Core progression into device physics |

### Additional learning resources

- **IEEE Xplore** for primary literature and standards
- **Open-source SDR, FPGA, and RISC-V ecosystems** for practical systems work
- **Vendor app notes and design guides** for circuit, FPGA, and power work
- **Cleanroom and foundry training resources** for device and VLSI tracks

## 8.5 Canonical Literature and Research Reading Roadmap

ECE is strongly paper-centered. A serious researcher should know the papers that shaped information theory, semiconductors, control, DSP, power, and modern hardware systems.

### A. Information, coding, and signals

1. **Nyquist, H. (1928), "Certain Topics in Telegraph Transmission Theory," *Transactions of the AIEE*.** Foundation for signaling limits.
2. **Shannon, C. E. (1948), "A Mathematical Theory of Communication," *Bell System Technical Journal*.** The single most important information-theory paper.
3. **Hamming, R. W. (1950), "Error Detecting and Error Correcting Codes," *Bell System Technical Journal*.** Foundational coding-theory work.
4. **Cooley, J. W., and Tukey, J. W. (1965), "An Algorithm for the Machine Calculation of Complex Fourier Series," *Mathematics of Computation*.** FFT landmark.
5. **Viterbi, A. J. (1967), "Error Bounds for Convolutional Codes and an Asymptotically Optimum Decoding Algorithm," *IEEE Transactions on Information Theory*.** Core decoding framework.
6. **Gallager, R. G. (1962), LDPC code papers.** Early foundation later revived for modern coding.

### B. Devices, transistors, and scaling

7. **Bardeen, J., and Brattain, W. H. (1948), transistor papers in *Physical Review*.** Launch of transistor electronics.
8. **Shockley, W. (1949), transistor theory papers in *Bell System Technical Journal*.** Early theoretical foundation.
9. **Moore, G. E. (1965), "Cramming More Components onto Integrated Circuits," *Electronics*.** Famous scaling observation.
10. **Dennard, R. H. et al. (1974), MOSFET scaling paper in *IEEE Journal of Solid-State Circuits*.** Fundamental CMOS scaling framework.
11. **Hisamoto, D. et al. (2000), FinFET paper in *IEEE Transactions on Electron Devices*.** Major post-planar device milestone.
12. **Taur, Y. (2002), CMOS scaling analyses.** Important modern scaling perspective.

### C. Control, estimation, and systems

13. **Kalman, R. E. (1960), "A New Approach to Linear Filtering and Prediction Problems," *Journal of Basic Engineering*.** Foundational estimation and state-space control paper.
14. **Zadeh, L. A. (1965), fuzzy-set paper.** Important later systems and control influence.
15. **Åström and coauthors' control-design work, along with robust-control literature from Doyle and others.** Essential bridge into modern control systems.

### D. Communications, wireless, and networks

16. **Foschini, G. J. (1996), MIMO capacity papers.** Foundation for modern multi-antenna wireless.
17. **Telatar, E. (1999), "Capacity of Multi-Antenna Gaussian Channels," *European Transactions on Telecommunications*.** Canonical MIMO information-theory result.
18. **Tse and Viswanath-era wireless capacity papers.** Central to modern communication theory.
19. **Polar and turbo coding milestone papers** by Arıkan and Berrou et al. for modern coding practice.

### E. Computing hardware, architecture, and modern systems

20. **Mead, C., and Conway, L. (1980), *Introduction to VLSI Systems*.** Transformative design methodology reference.
21. **Hennessy and Patterson's architecture work and related performance-analysis literature.** Canonical architecture framework.
22. **Google TPU architecture papers and modern accelerator papers.** Landmark hardware-ML systems direction.
23. **RISC-V foundational publications.** Important open-ISA milestone for modern computer engineering.

### F. Modern crossovers and current frontier

24. **Candes, Romberg, and Tao (2006), compressed-sensing papers.** Major signal-processing crossover.
25. **He, K. et al. (2016), deep residual learning paper.** Strong influence on image, signal, and hardware acceleration research.
26. **Vaswani, A. et al. (2017), "Attention Is All You Need."** Important for modern signal, communication, and accelerator design spillover.
27. **Recent wide-bandgap power-device reviews and inverter-dominated grid papers.** Essential for contemporary power engineering.
28. **Silicon photonics and chiplet-architecture reviews.** Important for current hardware-integration frontiers.

## 8.6 Open Problems and Research Frontiers

| Frontier | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Post-Moore scaling and energy-efficient computing** | Central for AI, data centers, edge devices, and semiconductor economics |
| **Reliable autonomy and learning-enabled control** | Hard problem at the intersection of control, AI, sensing, and safety |
| **Grid modernization and high-renewable power systems** | Important for electrification, resilience, and decarbonization |
| **6G, reconfigurable wireless, and integrated sensing** | Defines the next communications frontier |
| **Wide-bandgap and advanced semiconductor devices** | Critical for power conversion and RF systems |
| **Hardware security and trustworthy embedded systems** | Increasingly important in cyber-physical infrastructure |
| **AI accelerators, neuromorphic systems, and in-memory computing** | Core response to energy-intensive ML workloads |
| **Quantum communication, sensing, and quantum-hardware interfaces** | Emerging bridge between EE, physics, and computation |

### Active current directions

- **Chiplets and heterogeneous integration**
- **GaN/SiC power electronics**
- **Edge AI and TinyML systems**
- **Reconfigurable intelligent surfaces and mmWave / THz wireless**
- **Silicon photonics and optical interconnects**
- **Grid-forming inverters and power-system resilience**
- **RISC-V and domain-specific accelerators**
- **Bioelectronics and neural interfaces**

## 8.9 What a Full Picture of ECE Research Requires

A research-ready ECE roadmap should make clear:

1. how students move from **math, physics, and programming** into circuits, systems, communications, power, and computing hardware;
2. how the field depends on both **theory and lab implementation**, from proofs and transforms to chips, converters, and embedded prototypes;
3. how graduate training is shaped by **specialized toolchains, labs, conferences, and publication cultures**;
4. which **textbooks** anchor the core undergraduate sequence and the main graduate specializations;
5. which **papers** define information theory, transistors, scaling, estimation, wireless, and modern hardware systems;
6. and how ECE connects to **computing, energy, robotics, materials, and physics**.

That is what turns an ECE overview into a genuine roadmap toward research capability.
