# 30. Sociology

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Sociology, related social theory and policy majors |
| Masters | MA in Sociology, Demography, Social Research |
| PhD | Sociology with subfield specialization |

**Note:** Sociology studies social structure, inequality, institutions, culture, identity, and social change. It is methodologically plural, ranging from surveys and causal inference to ethnography and historical comparison.

---

## 30.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Intro sociology | Social structure, culture, institutions, deviance, inequality | Giddens & Sutton - *Sociology* |
| Social theory | Marx, Weber, Durkheim, Simmel, Bourdieu, Foucault, Giddens | Ritzer & Stepnisky - *Sociological Theory* |
| Research methods | Surveys, interviews, ethnography, comparative methods, mixed methods | Babbie - *The Practice of Social Research* |
| Social statistics | Regression, categorical data, multilevel basics | Agresti & Finlay - *Statistical Methods for the Social Sciences* |
| Stratification | Class, race, gender, mobility, poverty, wealth | Grusky - *Social Stratification* |
| Race and ethnicity | Racial formation, immigration, segregation, inequality | Omi & Winant - *Racial Formation in the United States* |
| Gender and sexuality | Feminist theory, work-family, identity, institutions | Lorber or related standard texts |
| Deviance and criminology | Labeling, social control, punishment, policing | Clinard & Meier or related standard texts |

### Graduate depth
- Stratification and mobility
- Race, ethnicity, and immigration
- Medical sociology and population health
- Culture, organizations, and economic sociology
- Political sociology and social movements
- Demography and family

## 30.2 Key Methods & Tools

| Domain | Core tools |
|---|---|
| Quantitative sociology | Surveys, panel data, event history, causal designs |
| Qualitative sociology | Ethnography, in-depth interviews, comparative-historical work |
| Computational sociology | Text analysis, digital trace data, social networks |
| Demography | Life tables, hazards, decomposition, projection |

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Classical sociology and social theory
- Marx K, Engels F. *The Communist Manifesto*.
- Durkheim E. 1893. *The Division of Labor in Society*.
- Durkheim E. 1897. *Suicide*.
- Weber M. 1905. *The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism*.
- Weber M. bureaucracy and domination writings.
- Simmel G. metropolis and money essays.

#### Interaction, identity, and social order
- Mead GH. *Mind, Self, and Society*.
- Goffman E. 1959. *The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life*.
- Goffman E. 1963. *Stigma*.
- Becker HS. 1963. *Outsiders*.
- Garfinkel H. 1967. *Studies in Ethnomethodology*.
- Hochschild AR. 1983. *The Managed Heart*.

#### Stratification, class, and mobility
- Blau PM, Duncan OD. 1967. *The American Occupational Structure*.
- Bourdieu P. 1984. *Distinction*.
- Wilson WJ. 1987. *The Truly Disadvantaged*.
- Massey DS, Denton NA. 1993. *American Apartheid*.
- Goldthorpe JH. class mobility studies.
- Chetty R et al. mobility atlas and neighborhood-opportunity papers.

#### Race, ethnicity, and inequality
- Du Bois WEB. 1903. *The Souls of Black Folk*.
- Myrdal G. 1944. *An American Dilemma*.
- Omi M, Winant H. 1986. *Racial Formation in the United States*.
- Bonilla-Silva E. 2003. *Racism without Racists*.
- Pager D. 2003. discrimination in hiring. *AJS*.
- Small ML, Pager D. 2020. sociological perspectives on race and discrimination. *Annual Review of Sociology*.

#### Culture, networks, and organizations
- Granovetter MS. 1973. Strength of weak ties. *AJS*.
- Granovetter M. 1985. Economic action and social structure. *AJS*.
- DiMaggio PJ, Powell WW. 1983. Iron cage revisited. *ASR*.
- Swidler A. 1986. Culture in action. *ASR*.
- Lamont M. symbolic boundaries papers.
- Zelizer V. relational work and social meaning of money studies.

#### Family, gender, and care
- Parsons T, Bales RF. family role writings as major historical references.
- West C, Zimmerman DH. 1987. Doing gender. *Gender & Society*.
- Ridgeway CL. status and gender papers.
- England P. gender inequality and labor-market studies.
- Hochschild AR, Machung A. *The Second Shift*.
- Edin K, Kefalas M. family and inequality ethnographic studies.

#### Medical sociology, population, and health
- Link BG, Phelan J. 1995. Social conditions as fundamental causes. *JHSB*.
- Phelan J, Link B, Tehranifar P. 2010. fundamental cause update. *JHSB*.
- Aneshensel CS. stress process literature.
- Mirowsky J, Ross CE. education and health papers.
- Marmot M. social gradient papers relevant to medical sociology.
- Cockerham WC. health lifestyle theory writings.

#### Political sociology, punishment, and social change
- Tilly C. social movements and contention writings.
- McAdam D. 1982. *Political Process and the Development of Black Insurgency*.
- Skocpol T. 1979. *States and Social Revolutions*.
- Wacquant L. punishment, urban marginality, and carceral state studies.
- Western B. incarceration and inequality papers.
- Putnam RD. 2000. *Bowling Alone*.

#### Methods and contemporary turns
- Abbott A. sequence analysis and process sociology papers.
- Small ML. 2009. how many cases do I need? *Ethnography*.
- Morgan SL, Winship C. causal inference in sociology literature.
- Salganik MJ. digital sociology and computational social science papers.
- Watts DJ. network cascades and online diffusion studies.
- Edelmann A, Wolff T, Montagne D, Bail CA. 2020. computational social science review. *Annual Review of Sociology*.

## 30.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- New forms of inequality under platform capitalism and AI
- Trust, polarization, and institutional legitimacy
- Climate migration, adaptation, and environmental justice
- Stronger causal inference without abandoning sociological theory
- Better integration of computational and ethnographic methods
- Care work, demographic aging, and changing family forms
