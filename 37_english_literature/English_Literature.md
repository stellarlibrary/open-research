## 37.1 Core Curriculum (Undergraduate)

### Core Courses

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Introduction to Literary Studies** | Genre, close reading, interpretation, argument, literary research |
| **British Literature Survey** | Medieval to contemporary periods, major genres and movements |
| **American Literature Survey** | Colonial writing to contemporary US and diasporic literature |
| **Poetry** | Prosody, form, lyric traditions, close analysis |
| **Shakespeare / Drama** | Tragedy, comedy, history plays, performance and adaptation |
| **Novel / Narrative** | Realism, modernism, postmodernism, narrative form and theory |
| **Literary Theory & Criticism** | Formalism, structuralism, poststructuralism, feminist, queer, Marxist, postcolonial, ecocritical approaches |
| **Research Seminar or Capstone** | Independent topic, scholarly bibliography, seminar paper or thesis |

### Common Electives
- Medieval literature, Renaissance, Romanticism, Victorian studies, modernism, contemporary literature, African American literature, world literature, children's literature, digital humanities, composition studies.

## 37.2 Graduate Specialization Areas

#### A. Period Studies
- Medieval, early modern, eighteenth century, Romantic, Victorian, modernist, post-1945, contemporary.

#### B. Theory & Criticism
- Aesthetics, narrative theory, critical theory, feminist and queer theory, psychoanalysis, deconstruction, Marxism.

#### C. Race, Empire & Global Anglophone Studies
- African diasporic, postcolonial, Indigenous, transnational, migration, world literature.

#### D. Rhetoric, Composition & Writing Studies
- Pedagogy, literacy, writing program administration, technical and professional communication, digital rhetoric.

#### E. Book History, Archives & Digital Humanities
- Print culture, textual scholarship, editorial theory, stylometry, corpus methods, digital archives.

---

## 37.3 Key Methods

| Method | Application |
|---|---|
| **Close Reading** | Attention to diction, form, voice, syntax, image, and structure |
| **Historicist Reading** | Situating texts in political, social, and intellectual context |
| **Theory-driven Interpretation** | Using feminist, Marxist, psychoanalytic, queer, postcolonial, or ecocritical lenses |
| **Archival / Bibliographic Method** | Manuscripts, editions, circulation, reception, print culture |
| **Narrative & Rhetorical Analysis** | Plot, focalization, genre, persuasion, audience |
| **Digital Humanities** | Text mining, stylometry, topic modeling, network analysis |

## 37.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **AI and authorship** - Machine-generated text, originality, labor, pedagogy, and the future of literary production.
- **Canon revision and expansion** - Balancing historical coverage with anti-exclusionary curricular redesign.
- **World literature and translation** - Moving beyond Anglocentric circulation models.
- **Digital humanities** - Scaling computation without losing philological and interpretive precision.
- **Public humanities and the profession** - Publishing, alternative careers, and the shrinking tenure-track market.
- **Climate and environmental humanities** - Narrative, scale, and representation in the Anthropocene.
