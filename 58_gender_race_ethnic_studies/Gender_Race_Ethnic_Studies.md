## 58.1 Core Curriculum

### Core Courses (across fields)

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Introduction to Gender & Sexuality Studies** | Gender construction, feminism, sexuality, queer and trans studies |
| **Introduction to Black / African American Studies** | Slavery, emancipation, diaspora, culture, politics, social thought |
| **Introduction to Ethnic Studies** | Race formation, migration, colonialism, comparative racialization |
| **Feminist Theory** | Liberal, radical, socialist, Black, postcolonial, transnational feminisms |
| **Critical Race Theory** | Law, institutions, whiteness, structural racism, racial capitalism |
| **Intersectionality** | Crenshaw's framework, methodology, coalition, policy analysis |
| **Queer / Trans Studies** | Performativity, sexuality history, embodiment, trans politics |
| **Research Methods** | Oral history, ethnography, discourse analysis, archives, community research |

## 58.2 Graduate Specialization Areas

#### A. Feminist & Gender Theory
- Reproduction, care, labor, embodiment, affect, science studies, disability.

#### B. Critical Race & Ethnic Studies

#### C. Sexuality and Queer / Trans Studies
- Intimacy, law, health, cultural production, transnational sexuality politics.

#### D. Media, Representation & Cultural Production
- Film, literature, visual culture, performance, digital publics.

#### E. Health, Environment & Science
- Reproductive justice, disability justice, environmental racism, technoscience.

## 58.3 Key Methods & Tools

| Method | Application |
|---|---|
| **Intersectional Analysis** | Studying how race, gender, class, sexuality, disability, and nation co-constitute power |
| **Archival Recovery** | Reconstructing marginalized histories and movements |
| **Oral History / Testimonio** | Community knowledge, memory, political voice |
| **Ethnography** | Everyday institutions, identity, activism, digital culture |
| **Critical Theory** | Ideology critique, discourse, power, representation |

## 58.5 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **Trans politics and state regulation** - Healthcare, law, identity documentation, and institutional access.
- **Racial capitalism and inequality** - Linking labor, housing, debt, policing, and extraction.
- **Algorithmic power** - Bias, surveillance, moderation, and digital public life.
- **Climate and environmental justice** - Disproportionate harms, sovereignty, displacement, and repair.
- **Abolition and safety** - Alternatives to carceral institutions and transformative justice models.
- **Method and coalition** - Turning intersectionality from slogan into rigorous research design.
