# 33. Geography

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Geography, GIS, Environmental Geography, Urban and Regional Studies |
| Masters | Geography, GIScience, Remote Sensing, Urban Planning |
| PhD | Geography across human, physical, and GIScience tracks |

**Note:** Geography is the spatial discipline linking environmental processes, human systems, and geospatial methods. It remains one of the clearest bridges between natural and social science in the university curriculum.

---

## 33.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Physical geography | Climate, landforms, soils, biogeography, hydrology | Christopherson & Birkeland - *Geosystems* |
| Human geography | Population, migration, urbanization, globalization, place | Knox & Marston - *Human Geography* |
| GIS | Vector/raster models, projections, overlay, network analysis | Bolstad - *GIS Fundamentals* |
| Remote sensing | Sensors, imagery, classification, change detection | Jensen - *Introductory Digital Image Processing* |
| Cartography | Map design, visualization, scale, typography | Slocum et al. - *Thematic Cartography and Geovisualization* |
| Research methods | Spatial statistics, field methods, qualitative geography | Gomez & Jones or equivalent methods texts |
| Geographic thought | Positivism, humanism, Marxism, feminist and postcolonial geography | Cresswell - *Geographic Thought* |

### Graduate depth
- Human and cultural geography
- Physical geography and climate/landscape systems
- GIScience and spatial data science
- Urban, political, and economic geography
- Environment-society and health geography

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Human-environment and geographic thought
- Humboldt A von. foundational physical geography and biogeography writings.
- Ritter C. comparative regional geography writings.
- Vidal de la Blache P. possibilist regional geography work.
- Sauer CO. 1925. morphology of landscape. *UC Publications in Geography*.
- Hartshorne R. 1939. *The Nature of Geography*.
- Schaefer FK. 1953. exceptionalism in geography. *Annals AAG*.
- Harvey D. 1969. *Explanation in Geography*.
- Relph E. 1976. *Place and Placelessness*.
- Cresswell T. place and mobility writings.

#### Quantitative, spatial, and GIScience revolutions
- Tobler WR. 1970. first law of geography. *Economic Geography*.
- Berry BJL. urban systems and quantitative geography writings.
- Hägerstrand T. innovation diffusion and time geography works.
- Cliff AD, Ord JK. spatial autocorrelation writings.
- Goodchild MF. 1992. geographical information science. *IJGIS*.
- Openshaw S. modifiable areal unit problem writings.
- Anselin L. 1995. local indicators of spatial association. *Geographical Analysis*.
- Fotheringham AS, Brunsdon C, Charlton M. geographically weighted regression framework.
- Goodchild MF. 2007. volunteered geographic information. *GeoJournal*.
- Elwood S. GIS, participation, and critical GIS writings.
- Miller HJ, Goodchild MF. data-driven geography papers.

#### Human geography, critical geography, and urban space
- Tuan YF. 1977. *Space and Place*.
- Lefebvre H. 1974. *The Production of Space*.
- Harvey D. 1973. *Social Justice and the City*.
- Massey D. 1994. *Space, Place and Gender*.
- Soja EW. 1989. *Postmodern Geographies*.
- Smith N. gentrification and uneven development writings.
- Castells M. urban question and network society writings.
- Brenner N, Schmid C. planetary urbanization papers. *IJURR*.
- McKittrick K. black geographies writings.

#### Political, economic, and cultural geography
- Wallerstein I. world-systems writings relevant to geography.
- Sack RD. territoriality writings.
- Agnew J. territorial trap. *Review of International Political Economy*.
- Gregory D. geopolitics and imaginative geographies texts.
- Harvey D. 1982. *The Limits to Capital*.
- Storper M. 1997. *The Regional World*.
- Appadurai A. 1996. *Modernity at Large* as a major mobility and global-cultural reference.
- Coe NM, Dicken P, Hess M. global production network papers.

#### Physical geography, climate, and earth observation
- Strahler AN. process geomorphology framework papers.
- Chorley RJ. systems approaches in physical geography.
- Budyko MI. climate-energy balance writings.
- Tucker CJ. NDVI and global vegetation monitoring studies.
- IPCC assessment reports as standard climate synthesis anchors.
- Running SW et al. MODIS/NPP and earth-system remote sensing studies.
- Lambin EF, Geist HJ. land-use and land-cover change synthesis.
- Turner BL II et al. land-change science framework papers. *PNAS*.

#### Health, justice, and applied geography
- Snow J. cholera mapping classic.
- Harley JB. deconstructing the map. *Cartographica*.
- Monmonier M. *How to Lie with Maps* as a core cartographic literacy reference.
- Meade MS. health geography synthesis texts and papers.
- Cutter SL. vulnerability and hazards geography writings.
- Walker G. environmental justice geography work.
- Kwan MP. feminist GIS and uncertainty papers.
- Crampton JW. critical cartography and surveillance geographies.
- Diez Roux AV. neighborhood effects and spatial health inequalities studies.

## 33.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- GeoAI and spatial prediction with interpretability
- Climate vulnerability mapping that is both rigorous and just
- Better integration of critical geography with technical GIScience
- Near-real-time earth observation for conservation and conflict monitoring
- Urban sustainability and uneven development under planetary urbanization
- Spatial privacy, surveillance, and platform geographies
