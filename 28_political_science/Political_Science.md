# 28. Political Science

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Political Science, Government, International Relations, Public Policy |
| Masters | MA in Political Science, IR, MPP, MPA |
| PhD | Political Science with subfield specialization |

**Note:** Political science combines empirical social science, historical interpretation, normative theory, and increasingly strong quantitative methods. Its major subfields are American politics, comparative politics, international relations, political theory, and methodology.

---

## 28.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Intro to political science | Power, legitimacy, institutions, regime types | Roskin et al. or Shepsle-type introductory frameworks |
| American politics | Congress, presidency, courts, parties, elections, federalism | Kernell et al. - *The Logic of American Politics* |
| Comparative politics | States, regimes, institutions, democratization | Clark, Golder & Golder - *Foundations of Comparative Politics* |
| International relations | Realism, liberalism, institutions, war, trade | Frieden, Lake & Schultz - *World Politics* |
| Political theory | Justice, liberty, equality, democracy, state power | Wolff - *An Introduction to Political Philosophy* |
| Methods | Research design, regression, qualitative inference, experiments | Kellstedt & Whitten - *The Fundamentals of Political Science Research* |

### Graduate depth
- American politics: behavior, institutions, race and ethnicity, law and courts
- Comparative politics: states, authoritarianism, democratization, development
- IR: security, IPE, organizations, war, climate politics
- Theory: canonical texts and contemporary normative theory
- Methods: causal inference, formal modeling, text-as-data, historical process tracing

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Political theory and the canon
- Aristotle. *Politics*.
- Machiavelli N. *The Prince*.
- Hobbes T. *Leviathan*.
- Locke J. *Second Treatise of Government*.
- Rousseau JJ. *The Social Contract*.
- Rawls J. 1971. *A Theory of Justice*.

#### Democracy, institutions, and representation
- Dahl RA. 1961. *Who Governs?*.
- Downs A. 1957. *An Economic Theory of Democracy*.
- Duverger M. 1954. *Political Parties*.
- Lipset SM. 1959. Some social requisites of democracy. *APSR*.
- Lijphart A. 1999. *Patterns of Democracy*.
- Przeworski A et al. 2000. *Democracy and Development*.

#### American politics and political behavior
- Campbell A et al. 1960. *The American Voter*.
- Converse PE. 1964. Nature of belief systems. *Ideology and Discontent*.
- Fenno RF. 1978. *Home Style*.
- Mayhew DR. 1974. *Congress: The Electoral Connection*.
- McCarty N, Poole KT, Rosenthal H. 2006. *Polarized America*.
- Gilens M, Page BI. 2014. Testing theories of American politics. *Perspectives on Politics*.

#### Comparative politics and state formation
- Moore B Jr. 1966. *Social Origins of Dictatorship and Democracy*.
- Skocpol T. 1979. *States and Social Revolutions*.
- Evans PB, Rueschemeyer D, Skocpol T. 1985. *Bringing the State Back In*.
- North DC, Wallis JJ, Weingast BR. 2009. *Violence and Social Orders*.
- Acemoglu D, Robinson JA. 2006. *Economic Origins of Dictatorship and Democracy*.
- Levitsky S, Way LA. 2010. *Competitive Authoritarianism*.

#### International relations and security
- Waltz KN. 1979. *Theory of International Politics*.
- Keohane RO. 1984. *After Hegemony*.
- Wendt A. 1992. Anarchy is what states make of it. *IO*.
- Fearon JD. 1995. Rationalist explanations for war. *IO*.
- Jervis R. 1978. Cooperation under the security dilemma. *World Politics*.
- Mearsheimer JJ. 2001. *The Tragedy of Great Power Politics*.

#### Political economy, conflict, and identity
- Olson M. 1965. *The Logic of Collective Action*.
- Ostrom E. 1990. *Governing the Commons*.
- Putnam RD. 1993. *Making Democracy Work*.
- Fearon JD, Laitin DD. 2003. Ethnicity, insurgency, and civil war. *APSR*.
- Posner DN. 2004. Ethnic politics and institutions. *APSR*.
- Cederman LE, Wimmer A, Min B. 2010. Why do ethnic groups rebel? *World Politics*.

#### Methods and modern empirical political science
- King G, Keohane RO, Verba S. 1994. *Designing Social Inquiry*.
- Brady HE, Collier D. 2004. *Rethinking Social Inquiry*.
- Imai K, King G, Stuart EA. matching and causal inference papers.
- Gerber AS, Green DP. field experimentation papers.
- Hopkins DJ. 2018. *The Increasingly United States*.
- Grimmer J, Stewart BM. 2013. Text as data review. *Political Analysis*.

## 28.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Democratic erosion and institutional resilience
- Polarization, distrust, and information environments
- AI governance, digital authoritarianism, and cyber conflict
- Climate politics and green industrial policy
- Better measurement of identity, representation, and state capacity
- Combining formal theory, qualitative depth, and credible causal designs
