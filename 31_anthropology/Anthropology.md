# 31. Anthropology

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Anthropology, Archaeology |
| Masters | Anthropology, Archaeology, Applied Anthropology, Museum Studies |
| PhD | Anthropology across four fields |

**Note:** Anthropology is traditionally organized around sociocultural anthropology, biological anthropology, archaeology, and linguistic anthropology. Its unifying commitment is comparative, field-based, and historically deep study of human life.

---

## 31.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Intro anthropology | Four fields, human diversity, evolution, material culture, language | Ember, Ember & Peregrine - *Anthropology* |
| Cultural anthropology | Kinship, exchange, ritual, power, globalization, fieldwork | Kottak - *Cultural Anthropology* |
| Biological anthropology | Human evolution, primates, variation, fossils, genetics | Jurmain et al. - *Introduction to Physical Anthropology* |
| Archaeology | Survey, excavation, dating, state formation, material analysis | Renfrew & Bahn - *Archaeology* |
| Linguistic anthropology | Language ideology, discourse, socialization, culture | Duranti - *Linguistic Anthropology* |
| Theory and ethnography | Classical and contemporary anthropological theory | McGee & Warms - *Anthropological Theory* |

### Graduate depth
- Sociocultural anthropology: medical, political, environmental, digital, STS
- Biological anthropology: primatology, bioarchaeology, human evolution, genetics
- Archaeology: regional specialization, heritage, materials, computational archaeology
- Linguistic anthropology: language socialization, ideology, documentation, revitalization

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Foundational theory and ethnography
- Tylor EB. 1871. *Primitive Culture*.
- Boas F. race, language, and culture essays.
- Malinowski B. 1922. *Argonauts of the Western Pacific*.
- Radcliffe-Brown AR. structural-functional writings.
- Mauss M. 1925. *The Gift*.
- Evans-Pritchard EE. 1940. *The Nuer*.

#### Structuralism, symbolism, and practice
- Lévi-Strauss C. 1949. *The Elementary Structures of Kinship*.
- Lévi-Strauss C. *The Savage Mind*.
- Geertz C. 1973. *The Interpretation of Cultures*.
- Turner V. ritual process writings.
- Bourdieu P. 1977. *Outline of a Theory of Practice*.
- Sahlins M. culture and practical reason writings.

#### Political, medical, and economic anthropology
- Wolf ER. 1982. *Europe and the People Without History*.
- Scott JC. 1985. *Weapons of the Weak*.
- Scheper-Hughes N. 1992. *Death without Weeping*.
- Farmer P. 1999. *Infections and Inequalities*.
- Kleinman A. illness narratives and explanatory models papers.
- Tsing AL. 2015. *The Mushroom at the End of the World*.

#### Biological anthropology and human evolution
- Darwin C. *The Descent of Man*.
- Washburn SL. new physical anthropology programmatic essay.
- Leakey and Johanson fossil reports on australopithecines and early Homo.
- Lovejoy CO. 1981. origins of human gait and posture papers.
- Stringer C, Andrews P. modern human origins debates.
- Reich D et al. ancient DNA studies on Neanderthal/Denisovan admixture.

#### Archaeology and material culture
- Childe VG. Neolithic and urban revolution writings.
- Binford LR. 1962. archaeology as anthropology. *AA*.
- Clarke DL. 1973. archaeology: loss of innocence. *Antiquity*.
- Hodder I. post-processual archaeology papers.
- Schiffer MB. formation process theory writings.
- Trigger BG. *A History of Archaeological Thought* as a core synthetic reference.

#### Linguistic anthropology and language ideologies
- Sapir E. language and culture essays.
- Whorf BL. language-thought writings.
- Hymes D. ethnography of speaking work.
- Silverstein M. language ideology papers.
- Ochs E, Schieffelin BB. language socialization papers.
- Irvine JT, Gal S. language ideology and differentiation. *Current Anthropology*.

#### Decolonization, reflexivity, and contemporary anthropology
- Clifford J, Marcus GE. 1986. *Writing Culture*.
- Abu-Lughod L. 1991. writing against culture. *Recapturing Anthropology*.
- Smith LT. 1999. *Decolonizing Methodologies*.
- TallBear K. indigenous STS and anthropology of genomics papers.
- Simpson A. sovereignty and refusal writings.
- Graeber D, Wengrow D. recent debates on social complexity and human history.

## 31.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Decolonizing fieldwork, archives, and museum practice
- Integrating genetics with archaeological and ethnographic interpretation
- Indigenous data sovereignty and collaborative methods
- Digital anthropology of platforms, AI, and infrastructure
- Climate change, mobility, and multispecies anthropology
- Faster, more ethical language documentation and revitalization
