# 40. Fine Arts & Art History

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | BFA in Studio Art, BA in Art History, BA in Visual Arts |
| Masters | MFA in Studio Art, MA in Art History, MA in Museum Studies |
| PhD | Art History, Visual Studies, Aesthetics |

---

## 40.1 Core Curriculum

### Studio Art (BFA)

| Area | Content |
|---|---|
| **Foundations** | Drawing, color, composition, 2D/3D design, critique |
| **Painting / Drawing** | Observation, abstraction, material handling, figure, installation links |
| **Sculpture / Ceramics** | Form, fabrication, casting, clay, site, object histories |
| **Printmaking / Photography** | Analog and digital workflows, editioning, image theory |
| **Video / New Media** | Time-based media, sound, interactive work, digital production |
| **Senior Thesis / Exhibition** | Cohesive body of work, artist statement, public presentation |

### Art History (BA/PhD)

| Area | Content |
|---|---|
| **Survey Sequence** | Ancient to contemporary art and architecture across regions |
| **Methods & Theory** | Formalism, iconography, social history, feminism, postcolonial and museum critique |
| **Research Seminar** | Object analysis, historiography, archives, curatorial writing |
| **Museum / Curatorial Practice** | Collection research, provenance, exhibition histories, conservation basics |

## 40.2 Key Methods & Sources

| Method | Use |
|---|---|
| **Studio Critique** | Iterative feedback, concept development, process articulation |
| **Formal Analysis** | Composition, line, color, scale, surface, medium |
| **Iconography / Visual Analysis** | Symbol, subject, motif, and interpretive context |
| **Archival / Provenance Research** | Exhibition history, ownership, catalogues, institutional records |
| **Material / Technical Study** | Medium, fabrication, conservation science, imaging |
| **Curatorial Method** | Selection, sequencing, interpretation, publics, display |

## 40.4 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **AI and image generation** - Authorship, training data, labor, and new studio workflows.
- **Provenance and restitution** - Colonial collecting, repatriation, and museum accountability.
- **Time-based media conservation** - Preserving software, video, and installation art.
- **Global modernisms** - Rewriting art history beyond Euro-American center-periphery models.
- **Publics and participation** - Social practice, community collaboration, and institutional critique.
- **Art markets and inequality** - Platformization, speculation, and access to cultural production.
