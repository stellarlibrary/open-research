# 55. Cognitive Science

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Cognitive Science, Cognitive and Brain Sciences |
| Masters | MA/MS in Cognitive Science, Cognitive Systems |
| PhD | Cognitive Science, Cognitive and Neural Systems, interdisciplinary psychology/neuroscience/AI tracks |

**Note:** Cognitive science is organized around the study of mind, representation, learning, language, and intelligence across psychology, neuroscience, AI, linguistics, philosophy, and anthropology.

---

## 55.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Intro cognitive science | History, levels of analysis, representations, models of mind | Bermúdez - *Cognitive Science* |
| Cognitive psychology | Attention, memory, categorization, reasoning, language | Goldstein - *Cognitive Psychology* |
| Neuroscience | Neural mechanisms of cognition | Bear, Connors & Paradiso - *Neuroscience* |
| AI and computation | Symbolic AI, ML, neural networks, planning | Russell & Norvig - *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* |
| Linguistics | Structure, meaning, acquisition, psycholinguistics | Fromkin et al. - *An Introduction to Language* |
| Philosophy of mind | Consciousness, intentionality, functionalism, embodiment | Clark - *Mindware* |
| Computational modeling | Bayesian models, RL, connectionism, cognitive architectures | Farrell & Lewandowsky or related standard texts |

### Graduate depth
- Perception and action
- Language and communication
- Memory, reasoning, and decision-making
- Computational cognitive science
- Cognitive neuroscience
- Development and cultural cognition

## 55.3 Foundational Texts

### Standard textbooks

| Subject | Text |
|---|---|

| Computational modeling | Farrell & Lewandowsky - *Computational Modeling of Cognition and Behavior* |
| Marr-level analysis | Marr - *Vision* |

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Foundational computational and cognitive frameworks
- Turing AM. 1950. computing machinery and intelligence. *Mind*.
- Wiener N. cybernetics writings relevant to cognition and control.
- Miller GA. 1956. magical number seven. *Psychological Review*.
- Chomsky N. 1956. three models for the description of language. *IRE Transactions on Information Theory*.
- Newell A, Simon HA. 1976. computer science as empirical inquiry and physical symbol system writings.
- Simon HA. bounded rationality writings.
- Marr D. 1982. *Vision*.
- Rumelhart DE, McClelland JL. 1986. *Parallel Distributed Processing*.
- Anderson JR. ACT-R and rational analysis papers.
- Elman JL. 1990. finding structure in time. *Cognitive Science*.

#### Perception, attention, and categorization
- Gibson JJ. ecological approach writings.
- Neisser U. perceptual cycle and early cognitive psychology writings.
- Treisman A, Gelade G. 1980. feature integration theory. *Cognitive Psychology*.
- Posner MI. cueing and attention studies.
- Shepard RN. universal law of generalization. *Science*.
- Rosch E. prototypes and basic-level categories papers.
- Palmer SE. perceptual organization and visual cognition writings.
- Biederman I. recognition-by-components work.
- Kersten D, Mamassian P, Yuille A. object perception as Bayesian inference. *Annual Review*.

#### Memory, reasoning, and decision-making
- Atkinson RC, Shiffrin RM. 1968. memory architecture.
- Baddeley AD, Hitch G. 1974. working memory.
- Tulving E. episodic vs semantic memory papers.
- Craik FIM, Lockhart RS. levels-of-processing framework.
- Rescorla RA, Wagner AR. 1972. associative learning model.
- Tversky A, Kahneman D. heuristics and biases papers.
- Wason PC. selection task and reasoning studies.
- Gigerenzer G. ecological rationality writings.
- Kahneman D. 2011. *Thinking, Fast and Slow* as a major synthetic reference.

#### Language and cognition
- Chomsky N. 1959. review of Skinner. *Language*.
- Fodor JA. modularity and language-of-thought writings.
- Lakoff G, Johnson M. 1980. *Metaphors We Live By*.
- Pinker S, Bloom P. natural language and natural selection debate. *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*.
- Clark HH. dialogue and joint action papers.
- Tomasello M. usage-based and shared-intentionality work.
- Elman JL. simple recurrent networks and language learning papers.
- Frank MC, Goodman ND. pragmatic inference and language understanding studies.
- Linzen T, Dupoux E, Goldberg Y. later neural-network grammar studies anchoring current language-model debates.

#### Development, culture, and embodiment
- Piaget J. developmental constructivism texts.
- Vygotsky LS. sociocultural cognition writings.
- Bruner JS. scaffolding and formats writings.
- Gopnik A, Wellman H. theory-of-mind and causal learning work.
- Elman JL et al. 1996. *Rethinking Innateness*.
- Clark A, Chalmers D. 1998. extended mind. *Analysis*.
- Barsalou LW. grounded cognition papers.
- Hutchins E. 1995. *Cognition in the Wild*.
- Henrich J, Heine SJ, Norenzayan A. 2010. WEIRD critique. *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*.

#### Cognitive neuroscience and brain systems
- Broca P. aphasia localization writings.
- Wernicke C. language-area localization writings.
- Hubel DH, Wiesel TN. receptive field studies in visual cortex.
- Milner B. H.M. and memory systems studies.
- O'Keefe J, Nadel L. hippocampus as cognitive map framework.
- Kanwisher N, McDermott J, Chun MM. fusiform face area study. *Journal of Neuroscience*.
- Rao RPN, Ballard DH. predictive coding in the visual cortex. *Nature Neuroscience*.
- Poldrack RA. reverse inference and neuroimaging interpretation papers.

#### Consciousness, brain, and current frontiers
- Baars BJ. global workspace theory writings.
- Tononi G. integrated information theory papers.
- Dehaene S, Changeux JP. conscious processing and global neuronal workspace papers.
- Clark A. predictive processing and embodied mind writings.
- Friston K. free-energy and predictive-processing framework papers.
- Krakauer JW et al. neuroscience needs behavior critique. *Neuron*.
- Lake BM, Ullman TD, Tenenbaum JB, Gershman SJ. building machines that learn like people. *BBS*.
- Binz M, Schulz E. using foundation models in cognitive science reviews.

## 55.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Whether modern AI systems are useful cognitive models
- Consciousness theories and stronger adversarial testing
- Grounding, embodiment, and meaning in language and concept learning
- Better bridges between neural data and computational theory
- More cross-cultural cognitive science beyond WEIRD samples
- Unified models linking language, perception, memory, and action
