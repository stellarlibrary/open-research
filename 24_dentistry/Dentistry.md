# 24. Dentistry

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Pre-dental preparation | Biology, chemistry, organic chemistry, physics, biochemistry |
| Professional degree | DDS, DMD |
| Postgraduate training | GPR/AEGD, specialty residencies |
| Research graduate study | Oral biology, craniofacial biology, dental materials |

**Note:** Dentistry is a professional health field centered on oral diagnosis, prevention, restoration, surgery, and long-term maintenance. Training combines biomedical science, simulation, procedural practice, and high-volume patient care.

---

## 24.1 Core Curriculum

| Course | Main topics | Standard text |
|---|---|---|
| Head and neck anatomy | Cranial nerves, fascial spaces, vasculature, TMJ | Fehrenbach & Herring - *Illustrated Anatomy of the Head and Neck* |
| Dental anatomy and occlusion | Tooth morphology, eruption, occlusion | Nelson & Ash - *Wheeler's Dental Anatomy, Physiology and Occlusion* |
| Operative dentistry | Caries removal, restorations, adhesives | Ritter, Boushell & Walter - *Sturdevant's Art and Science of Operative Dentistry* |
| Periodontics | Periodontal disease, scaling, surgery, implants | Newman, Takei, Klokkevold & Carranza - *Carranza's Clinical Periodontology* |
| Endodontics | Pulp biology, root canal therapy, periapical disease | Hargreaves & Berman - *Cohen's Pathways of the Pulp* |
| Prosthodontics | Fixed and removable prostheses, occlusion, implants | Rosenstiel, Land & Fujimoto - *Contemporary Fixed Prosthodontics* |
| Oral pathology | Mucosal disease, cysts, tumors, salivary lesions | Neville et al. - *Oral and Maxillofacial Pathology* |
| Oral radiology | Periapical, panoramic, CBCT interpretation | White & Pharoah - *Oral Radiology* |
| Pediatric dentistry | Caries prevention, behavior management, pulp therapy | Dean, Avery & McDonald - *McDonald and Avery's Dentistry for the Child and Adolescent* |

### Graduate depth
- Orthodontics and dentofacial orthopedics
- Oral and maxillofacial surgery
- Endodontics, periodontics, prosthodontics
- Oral medicine, oral pathology, oral radiology
- Dental public health and biomaterials

### Recommended papers and landmark studies

#### Caries, prevention, and cariology
- Miller WD. 1890. Chemo-parasitic theory and oral microbiology foundations.
- Stephan RM. 1940. pH changes after glucose rinse. *JADA*.
- Keyes PH. 1960. Infectious and transmissible nature of experimental dental caries. *Archives of Oral Biology*.
- Dean HT and fluoridation-era papers on caries prevention and fluorosis remain foundational public-health readings.
- Fejerskov O. Major papers on de/remineralization and caries as a dynamic process.
- Featherstone JDB. Caries-balance / minimally invasive cariology papers.
- ten Cate JM. Remineralization and lesion-biology papers.
- Marinho VCC et al. Fluoride-toothpaste Cochrane reviews.
- Frencken JE et al. Atraumatic restorative treatment foundational papers.
- Gao SS et al. Silver diamine fluoride caries-arrest reviews. *J Dent Res*.

#### Periodontology and implants
- Löe H, Theilade E, Jensen SB. 1965. Experimental gingivitis in man. *J Periodontology*.
- Axelsson P, Lindhe J. Preventive periodontology and maintenance-trial papers.
- Lindhe J, Nyman S. Classic periodontal therapy and maintenance papers.
- Socransky SS et al. 1998. Subgingival microbial complexes. *J Clin Periodontol*.
- Pihlstrom BL, Michalowicz BS, Johnson NW. 2005. Periodontal diseases. *Lancet*.
- Albrektsson T et al. 1981. Criteria for osseointegrated implants. *Acta Orthop Scand*.
- Brånemark PI et al. Classic osseointegration clinical reports.
- Berglundh T et al. Peri-implantitis experimental and review papers.
- Tonetti MS et al. 2018. New classification of periodontal diseases. *J Clin Periodontol*.

#### Adhesive and restorative dentistry
- Black GV. Classical operative-dentistry and cavity-preparation principles.
- Buonocore MG. 1955. Acid-etch technique. *J Dent Res*.
- Bowen RL. 1963. Bis-GMA resin development papers.
- Nakabayashi N, Kojima K, Masuhara E. 1982. Hybrid-layer bonding to dentin.
- Fusayama T. Total-etch and caries-detection papers.
- Mount GJ, Tyas MJ. Minimal-intervention dentistry papers.
- Opdam NJM et al. Restoration-longevity practice studies.
- Demarco FF et al. Direct-restoration survival reviews. *J Dent Res*.

#### Endodontics and pulp biology
- Kakehashi S, Stanley HR, Fitzgerald RJ. 1965. Role of bacteria in pulp-exposure outcomes. *Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol*.
- Schilder H. 1967. Cleaning and shaping the root canal. *Dent Clin North Am*.
- Sundqvist G. Bacteriology of infected root-canals papers.
- Bystrom A, Sundqvist G. Irrigation and instrumentation papers.
- Sjögren U et al. Success factors in endodontic treatment papers.
- Ng YL, Mann V, Gulabivala K. Outcome of primary root-canal treatment review. *Int Endod J*.
- Ricucci D, Siqueira JF Jr. Apical-periodontitis histobacteriology papers.

#### Orthodontics, growth, and craniofacial science
- Angle EH. Classic malocclusion classification.
- Broadbent BH. Introduction of cephalometrics.
- Tweed CH. Extraction and cephalometric treatment-philosophy papers.
- Enlow DH. Craniofacial-growth theory writings.
- Proffit WR. Equilibrium theory and treatment-effect syntheses.
- Moss ML. Functional-matrix hypothesis papers.
- Little RM. Long-term stability and mandibular-incisor crowding papers.

#### Oral medicine, pathology, and oral-systemic health
- Neville BW and Regezi JA pathology-defining review chapters and studies remain curricular anchors.
- Warnakulasuriya S. Oral potentially malignant disorders reviews. *Oral Oncology*.
- Lockhart PB et al. Infective-endocarditis prophylaxis guidance papers.
- Slade GD and collaborators. Oral-health-related quality-of-life / OHIP papers.
- Tonetti MS, Van Dyke TE. Periodontitis and atherosclerotic disease workshop reports. *J Periodontol*.
- Peres MA et al. 2019. Oral diseases as a global public-health challenge. *Lancet*.
- Petersen PE. WHO oral-health reports and global-burden syntheses.

## 24.4 Open Problems & Frontiers

- Durable bioactive restorative materials
- Better peri-implantitis prevention and treatment
- Earlier oral cancer detection and salivary diagnostics
- More conservative caries management at scale
- Digital workflows that improve quality, not just speed
- Better evidence on oral-systemic mechanisms
