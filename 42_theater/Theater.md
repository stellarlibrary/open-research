# 42. Theater & Performing Arts

## Degree Programs

| Level | Programs |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Theater, Acting, Musical Theater, Design/Technology, Dance, Playwriting |
| Masters | MFA in Acting, Directing, Playwriting, Design, Dance; MA in Theater Studies |

---

## 42.1 Core Curriculum

### Performance Track (BFA Acting)

| Course | Content |
|---|---|
| **Acting Technique** | Stanislavski-derived systems, scene study, objective/action, improvisation |
| **Voice & Speech** | Breath, resonance, text work, dialects, IPA |
| **Movement** | Alexander, Laban, stage combat, physical theater, dance for actors |
| **Script Analysis** | Dramatic structure, beat work, genre, dramaturgical context |
| **Theater History** | Greek through contemporary performance traditions |
| **Directing / Devising** | Staging, rehearsal process, concept work, collaboration |
| **Production Lab** | Performance or crew assignments, rehearsal discipline, backstage practice |

### Design & Technical Track (BFA)

| Area | Content |
|---|---|
| **Scenic / Costume / Lighting / Sound Design** | Drafting, rendering, visual research, cueing, technical systems |
| **Stage Management / Technical Direction** | Scheduling, calling shows, safety, budgeting, shop and production management |
| **Dramaturgy / Playwriting** | Research packets, adaptation, structure, workshop development |

## 42.2 Key Methods & Tools

| Method | Application |
|---|---|
| **Rehearsal-based Inquiry** | Iterative embodied testing of interpretation |
| **Performance Analysis** | Gesture, blocking, liveness, spectatorship, affect |
| **Dramaturgical Research** | Historical context, production history, adaptation, source work |
| **Devising / Collaborative Creation** | Ensemble-generated text, movement, and structure |
| **Design Visualization** | Drafting, model building, rendering, lighting plots, sound cueing |
| **Practice-as-Research** | Creative work as research output in MFA and PhD contexts |

## 42.4 Open Problems & Research Frontiers

- **Liveness and mediation** - Streaming, hybrid performance, and the changing ontology of theater.
- **Equity and canon revision** - Casting, institutional access, authorship, and decolonizing repertories.
- **Immersive and site-specific work** - Participation, ethics, design, and audience agency.
- **Performance and politics** - Documentary theater, protest, testimony, and social practice.
- **Technology in production** - Motion capture, AI-assisted tools, digital scenography, spatial sound.
- **Economic sustainability** - Labor precarity, nonprofit models, touring, and post-pandemic production ecology.
